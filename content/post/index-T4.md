# 826

「……I see. It’s true that many of the guerrilla’s aren’t soldiers. They are adventurers and mercenaries. We can’t stop them from going with you out of their own volition. And, if a soldier decides to leave the noble they serve, they aren’t any issues as long as they do so properly either. ……It’s unfortunate for us though.」

Theorem replied to Rei’s question.

Both armies were now gathered at the Mercurio’s army’s camp as they busily carried out the various order they were given.

In the middle of all this, Rei had asked Theorem if the guerrillas that wanted to follow him would be allowed to go to Gilm……the Mireana Kingdom. Surprisingly, Theorem agreed, making Rei, who had expected him to be reluctant, quite surprised.

「That’s a surprise. I didn’t think you’d allow it so easily.」

「Forcing someone who wants to follow a particular individual to stay would likely cause unnecessary trouble. Besides, the final decision is up to Prince Mercurio. All I can do is put in a good word for you. ……Ah, please use the supplies brought in from the main camp for your troops. That’s right, especially the food. And as for the White Rose Knights…… Oh, I see. That should be fine. What about Prince Shurus’ cavalry? Go do that.」

Theorem continued to issue orders as he conversed with Rei.

Seeing that, Rei thought to himself that he would never be able to manage something like that.

「I’m not just saying this out of kindness. If there’s someone in the Mireana Kingdom who knows the Bestir Empire well as well, perhaps we could avoid resorting to rough tactics immediately whenever problems arise……right?」

「That is true. ……However, if I take the Bestir Empire’s side too often, I might be looked down on back in Gilm, so we still need to keep things in moderation.」

「I understand. I’ll be careful about it. But……even if they were to go to the Mireana Kingdom, won’t there need to be a lot of preparations? There will be quite a large group, right? Ah, you can talk to Prince Shurus’ adjutant about that.」

Rei gave a slight nod in response to Theorem’s words.

Some members of the guerrilla unit were married and some had families.

Many were also not married but had partners.

They wouldn’t all go to Gilm with Rei, but some definitely would.

In other words, they would need and escort on their way back to Gilm.

Rei had initially planned to just hop on Set and head back to the Mireana Kingdom……to Gilm, but if members of the guerrilla unit were coming with him, he couldn’t just abandon them.

(It will probably take us a while to get back. But, considering Rhodes’ situation, maybe it’s not that bad.)

Rhodes had still yet to wake from his coma.

Rei was still a little unsure of how he was supposed to bring him back to Gilm.

Set’s uniqueness meant that there were significant limitations in carrying anyone other than Rei on his back.

He could still handle a light adult woman or two children, but anything more and it would be hard for him to fly properly.

On the other hand, Set was more than capable of carrying weights that other Griffons wouldn’t be able to manage with his beak and claws.

An ordinary Griffon could easily fly with even two or three adults on their back, so this seemed to be a problem unique to Set, who had been created with the Magic Beast Art.

In any case, Rei had been considering the worst case scenario of wrapping Rhodes in a cloth of sorts and dangling him from Set’s claws as they flew back.

However, doing something like that would definitely have negative impacts on Rhodes’ health.

Especially in this season, when snow could start to fall at any time.

In such a situation, flying through the air, which would be much colder than on the ground, would be greatly detrimental to Rhodes.

As a result, Rei was actually quite grateful for the opportunity to be given a ride back to Gilm in a carriage.

(My only and greatest miscalculation……was the Gamelions in the end.)

Speaking with an adventurer from the guerrilla unit, he had learned that Gamelions would appear on the outskirts of the Bestir Empire.

However, he couldn’t priorities his appetite and leave behind Rhodes as he was. And, with those wanting to head back with him, he didn’t have the luxury of going hunting on his own.

If they missed the timing, snow would start to fall and they would have to wait until spring before making the trip back to the Bestir Empire.

Of course, even with snow, Rei would be fine with his Dragon Robe and Set didn’t care as a Griffon.

But he couldn’t do that to the cost of everyone else.

「I guess I’ll just have to give up.」

「Hm? What do you mean?」

Theorem, who had been giving more orders to his subordinates, turned around at Rei’s words.

「No, actually, I had been wanting to hunt some Gamelions after the end of the civil war to collect some meat. It’s about time for them to be in season, right?」

「……I’m not sure we can call them ‘in season’ when talking about those monsters.」

Theorem gave a wry smile as he spoke.

Gamelions were a C rank monster after all, which meant that a C rank party would be barely capable of matching a single one.

If a group of rookie adventurers tried working together to fight one, they would likely be crushed.

In that sense, Theorem wasn’t wrong.

(Although, Rei has a title and Set is an A rank monster……no, as a rare species, he would be considered S rank, so a C rank monster would certainly be no problem.)

As he thought of that, Theorem suddenly realised that he had been too preoccupied to give Rei any advice about that.

「Rei, about Set……I’ve heard rumours that he’s a rare species of Griffon.」

「I guess so.」

Set had used his skills freely after Rei had been cornered by Noiz.

Naturally, he didn’t think it would be possible to still keeps things a secret.

That was why he had Set use his skills brazenly after that.

「But, would anyone really try anything after knowing all that? After all, they’ve seen Set and my strength.」

The punitive force had literally experienced Set and Rei’s strength.

As for Mercurio’s army, Rei and Set were considered their greatest asset, so no one with any common sense would try to mess with them.

Theorem shook his head at Rei’s words.

「The nobles who were on the battlefield naturally wouldn’t try something so foolish. However, there are nobles in the Bestir Empire who did not participate in the civil, were unable to participate, or were not allowed to participate. Of course, they will here about you and Set’s abilities from those who witnessed it, but they won’t be able to fully grasp your strength.

「……So, you’re saying there’s a chance those kinds of people might try something?」

「Unfortunately. Of course, anyone with even the slightest intelligence wouldn’t try anything. But there are always those with ears that only hear what suits them and rejects whatever they don’t like to hear.」

As Theorem said that, Rei thought back to the various nobles he had met……not the ones close to him, like Elena, but the typical nobles who always believed they were right. Perhaps it was only natural for Rei to think of them.

「You mean they might try to take direct action against me?」

「Yes. Probably……no, I’m almost certain they will. But I want to tell you in advance so that you don’t think they represent the Bestir Empire as a whole. Prince Mercurio, Princess Frizione, and I are grateful to you Rei and we have no intentions of being hostile.」

Rei could tell from his tone that Theorem wasn’t lying.

Indeed, the Bestir Empire had just come out of civil war and their national power had certainly decreased as a result of it.

In such a situation, Theorem didn’t want to anger anyone like Rei and Set, with their high attack and mobility.

If Rei were determined to do so, he could realistically attack any city which didn’t have a form of protection against magic attacks.

His signature fire tornado could easily tear through small towns and villages, burning everything to ashes.

Furthermore, with his newly acquired Fire Emperor’s Crimson Armour, he could easily take out an entire army on his own.

To stop someone like Rei, they would have to bring in someone like Noiz, and even if they did, the damage to the surrounding area made Rei someone they’d rather not make an enemy of.

(Besides……if we really were to fight with Rei, Vihera-sama would undoubtedly side with him.)

Of course, between her family and the man she loved, Vihera would likely be quite conflicted about it.

But, if things started with the Bestir Empire attacking Rei, it would be clear who was at fault and Vihera would definitely side with Rei.

It was undoubtedly due to Rei’s efforts in the civil war that Mercurio had gained victory before the end of the year.

That is not to say they would have lost without Rei’s help, but if Theorem had been fighting alone, the civil war would almost certainly have dragged into the new year.

No, in the worst case, it might even have dragged on several years past that, not just into the new year.

If the civil war went on for that long, it would have been highly likely that other countries would have made their own moves, even with the Imperial Army serving as a deterrent.

As for the Mireana Kingdom, although the Neutral and Aristocratic factions were secretly helping them, the Royalist faction, the largest of the three in the Mireana Kingdom, would likely have made a move as well.

The fact that the Royalist faction had been contacted by various vassal states and remnants of conquered countries in the Bestir Empire had been conveyed to Theorem by Elena through Rei.

「In other words, no matter how I deal with those who attack us, the Bestir Empire won’t say anything?」

「That’s right. Of course, if you go too far, we will have to take action as well.」

「……Such as?」

Theorem quickly answered Rei’s question by writing something on a piece of paper.

「For example……if you killed all the family members of a noble who attacked you.」

「……Who do you think I am?」

「You’re Crimson, right? You used a fire tornado and Fire Emperor’s Crimson Armour to completely destroy the punitive force.」

That was all true and Rei had no words in response.

It had happened in the Spring War and the civil war after all.

With how many people he had killed, he really didn’t have the grounds to retort.

Of course, if he still held the mentality of an ordinary Japanese person, he might have felt anguished at the thought of taking a life……but Zepairu had helped with with adapting to this world.

So even when he did kill his enemies, he only felt a little pain.

(Hm? Thinking of it that way, what about Kabajid? He’s definitely from Japan, so it wouldn’t be surprising for him to have an aversion to killing as well. That is, unless he was like me and had that taken care of for him. ……Or maybe that was all sorted out from him growing up as a member of the royal family?)

Rei tilted his head as he thought about that, but it wasn’t something he could ask Theorem given the circumstances, so he changed the subject.

「So, for now, I can consider the civil war to be over, right?」

「Hm? Yes, that’s right. The civil war was ended quickly thanks to your help.」

「So, I can look forward to my reward?」

「That’s right, you can look forward to it. I will prepare a reward that suits your efforts. ……I’m thinking of a magic item that will probably be quite useful to you. However, I won’t be able to give it to you until after the victory parade is over. Is that okay?」

Rei thought for a moment before nodding.

# 827

That night on the Brissa Plains, as Mercurio’s army and the punitive force set up camp next to each other, the soldiers patrolling their respective camps were quite tense, knowing that fighting could still potentially break out at any moment.

There were some people who had suggested that the two armies should set up camp together, but their opinion was eventually rejected as being too premature to implement at this time, resulting in the two armies camping next to each other instead.

An additional metal fence had been set up between the two camps, aside from the one keeping monsters and bandits from entering, as both sides were yet to be able to fully trust each other.

In fact, many people openly spoken that they couldn’t forgive the punitive force for surrendering to the Mercurio army, even if the surrender was only for show.

Even on the side of Mercurio’s army, there were some who believed that since they had won, they could do whatever they wanted to the losers.

If such people were to clash with each other, fighting would quickly break out again, so both the Mercurio’s army and the punitive force were patrolling their camps carefully.

「Still, it’s good that it’s nearing winter now. There’s no need to rush to clean up the bodies.」

「I know, right? ……If it had been summer, we’d be hurrying to gather and cremate the bodies.」

Two soldiers who were patrolling Mercurio army’s camp exchanged words with each other as they carefully observed their surroundings.

While they were speaking in a relaxed tone, their eyes were still sharp.

They were determined not to miss anything as they walked through the camp, lit up by bonfires and the moonlight.

「But at this rate, it’ll be a while before we will get back to the Imperial Capital. This head on clash with so many soldiers means that the death toll was pretty high.」

「Yeah……that’s true. But, that’s just the main battlefield. Apparently, both sides had sent out small units to attack each other’s main camp without being seen.」

「Really? I didn’t hear about that at all.」

「Well, of course. They were all elite units. Even if the attacks were fended off, the losses wouldn’t have been small. ……Well, since the soldiers sent out for that were smaller in number, the losses wouldn’t have been excessive either. I guess they also didn’t want to show the bodies of so many elite soldiers to everyone.」

「Why not? If we were going to show any corpses, it would be the corpses of the punitive force that attacked us, right? So there’s no need to worry about that then, right?」

「That’s a big deal, you idiot.」

Hearing his partner’s seemingly thoughtless words, the other soldier sighed.

「Those elite soldiers represent the strongest fighters in the army……although that’s not quite accurate.」

The soldier sighed again as he thought of Rei and Set.

「They are the strongest fighters in the army. What do you think will happen if their corpses are shown to people?」

「……What will happen?」

「Haaah. I really was stupid to expect a good answer from you. Anyway, getting back to the main point, we can’t return to the Imperial Capital until we’ve sorted out most of the corpses. At least we low ranking soldiers won’t be able to head back first.」

「Does that mean the higher ups will be heading back to the Imperial Capital right away?」

「Yes. But, it’s not because they think it’s too much trouble to stay here. It’s because of time. The Bestir Empire’s national strength has suffered greatly from this civil war and all the important nobles are here on the battlefield. In particular, there a current and future heads of families present. With them being absent for so long, there is a high chance that more disturbances could break out. It might be different for us if we can quickly dispose of all the bodies though.」

「……As expected. Power struggles are a noble’s job after all.」

As his partner gave an annoyed sigh, the soldier nodded back with a wry smile.

For nobles, it was almost an instinct to desire more power.

He nodded knowing that.

Of course, this wasn’t the case for a lot of the nobles in Mercurio’s army.

After all, they were supporting the Third Prince, who wasn’t exactly in the best of positions before the civil war, so they were a rather unique lot.

In comparison, there were quite a number of ambitious nobles in Frizione’s faction.

「……Still, it’s probably a lot better than over there.」

The soldier muttered to himself as he looked over towards the punitive force’s camp.

Many of the nobles from Kabajid’s faction had remained to give orders to their subordinates, keeping the punitive force together somehow.

In addition, they were following Kabajid’s final instructions to obey Mercurio’s orders.

But, in Shurus’ case……some of the nobles in his faction had already fled back to their own territories while those that remained were trying to escape punishment by trying to use their noble privileges.

In a way, this was only natural.

After all, the Second Prince’s faction had been made up of a lot of nobles who had been rejected by the First Prince’s faction to begin with.

Of course, there were also many nobles in the Second Prince’s faction who were loyal to Shurus.

But, in such a situation, it was only natural for those who fled back to their own territories to stand out more.

After hearing of this, Shurus lamented that he should have sent more of them with the first punitive force to eliminate them.

Many of those nobles who had been captured by Mercurio’s army back then had been brought here as well.

They had been thrown over to where the prisoners were held and it was necessary for their situation to be discussed by Mercurio’s army and the punitive force as well.

Even if the same level of ransom couldn’t be demanded from them as the nobles who had taken part in this final battle, ransom still needed to be paid.

Negotiations over such matters were still underway despite it being night time and the camp wasn’t completely quiet as a result.

Many others were unable to sleep due to the adrenaline from the biggest battle of the civil war keeping them awake.

If taverns or brothels had been available in the camp, such people would have been able to gather together rowdily. However, all such facilities had left well before the final battle.

All the soldiers could do was drink what alcohol they had as they chatted excitedly with their fellow soldiers.

Naturally, many were also exhausted from the day’s fighting and were already fast asleep.

It was in such a situation, where even the smallest incident could lead to more fighting, that the two soldiers continued their patrol around the camp.

「……You want me to follow you guys? No matter the reason, changing my allegiance so soon after Prince Kabajid’s death would be extremely shameful. I refuse.」

「That’s right. I won’t change my master so easily either. How about you, Sobble?」

Both Bratta and Pelfiel immediately refused Theorem’s offer. Pelfiel called out to Sobble, the last of the trio present, urging him to respond.

They probably expected Sobble to refuse immediately as well.

That was what they thought, but to their surprise, Sobble stayed silent.

「Hey, Sobble. Did something happen to you while you were captured?」

Bratta asked with narrowed eyes.

If Sobble were to forget his gratitude to Kabajid at a time like this, Bratta would smack him over the head to bring him back to his senses.

That was Bratta had planned to do when he questioned Sobble, but Sobble finally replied with a voice as calm as ever.

「I too have no intention of abandoning my loyalty to Prince Kabajid so simply. But, isn’t Prince Kabajid dead? We have to decide what to do next in this situation. ……Do you think Prince Kabajid would be pleased if we stubbornly refused to cooperate with Prince Mercurio?」

「That is……」

For better or worse, Kabajid had been someone who valued efficiency and results.

That much could be seen from the fact that someone like Bratta had been promoted to a position as a close aide.

Of course, while Kabajid did value efficiency and results, that didn’t mean he tolerated excessively cruel acts for the sake of efficiency and results.

Regardless, that was why Sobble believed that they would be disappointing Kabajid if they were to stubbornly remain loyal to him and delay the restoration of the Bestir Empire as a result.

At Sobble’s explanation, neither Bratta or Pelfiel could deny that.

But despite knowing that, when asked if they could immediately switch their allegiance to the person who had killed their master……or more accurately, caused their master to kill themselves, they couldn’t bring themselves to agree.

They understood the reasoning, but their hearts couldn’t accept it.

「……I’m sorry, but even if it means being disloyal to Prince Kabajid, I don’t think I can serve Prince Mercurio any time soon.」

「Me too. No, rather, I don’t want to work under anyone other than Prince Kabajid. ……Besides, there are people I need to defeat. Two people in fact.」

The two people Bratta was referring to was immediately clear to Theorem, considering who he had been defeated and taken prisoner by, and who had defeated him in a previous battle without him being able to fight back.

「I’ll say this first, Rei will be returning to the Mireana Kingdom after the victory parade. Count Obrisin will just return to his territory though.」

That said, there was still a possibility that Gurgast would find even the victory parade too much of a bother and not join it at all.

Knowing Gurgast, Theorem was somewhat resigned to the fact that he would just do whatever he wanted.

He was also considering asking Vihera and Tilleyle to join the parade, but both of them were 50/50 at best.

「What-!? Both of them!?」

「Well yeah. I hired Rei to help out in the civil war, and now that it’s over, I don’t think he intends to stay in the Bestir Empire any longer. ……Well, to be honest, I’d be more than happy to have someone like Rei stay here.」

「……I guess so. He’s a man of such strength. If he could use that strength for the Bestir Empire, he would a be a huge force.」

Pelfiel murmured to herself as she recalled the battle where she had been one-sidedly beaten……despite Rei blatantly holding back.

Pelfiel was someone who held confidence in her own strength. She didn’t think herself to be the strongest fighter, but she still prided herself on the fact that she ranked among the strongest.

However……that confidence, pride, and even strength were all shattered in one single exchange.

She had only taken one hit, but that hit was enough to convince her that she would never be able to defeat Rei no matter how hard she tried.

If someone of that strength could be their ally, how reassuring would that be?

(Perhaps he’d even join me in training. Would thinking that be a betrayal of Prince Kabajid? No, it wouldn’t be. If Prince Kabajid were still alive, I think he would even support my idea. It would ultimately be to our benefit.)

As she thought to herself, Pelfiel even felt a sense of admiration for Rei, who was far stronger that she was.

Younger than her? An adventurer with no background? Smaller than her? None of that mattered.

Pelfiel placed the greatest importance on self improvement and becoming stronger. That was all that mattered to her.

「What is regrettable is that you only see his strength as an individual, not as a commander leading subordinates.」

This was where the people present different from others, like Gurgast and Vihera, who placed greater importance on individual strength.

After all, Pelfiel pursued not just individual strength but also strength as a leader.

However, Theorem shook his head at Pelfiel’s words.

「As you probably know already, Rei is an adventurer, not a soldier. While he might be able to coordinate his actions with a few companions, he’s not used to leading people in battle……that I’m sure of. At the very least, I’ve never seen him lead subordinates in battle in a conventional manner.」

Indeed, while the guerrilla unit was technically under Rei’s command, it was Pernyx who truly led them.

The only mission where Rei had worked together with Rei had been the night attack against the second punitive force led by Bratta and Soble. Even then, Rei and Set had gone to create a fire tornado while the guerrillas attacked the fleeing soldiers with bows and arrows.

To the guerrillas, Rei was more of a figurehead rather than an actual leader.

……Of course, that figurehead was extremely powerful and, as a result, made the guerrilla unit appear far stronger than they actually were.

「Let me……think over it.」

In the end, that was all Pelfiel said, leaving Sobble as the only one committed to following Mercurio.

# 828

Two days had passed since the end of the civil war. As with previous days, Mercurio’s army and the punitive force were still clearing away the bodies.

With so many people working together, the process was quick and most of the corpses on the Brissa Plain had already been collected.

……Of course, that excluded the bodies that had been eaten or damaged by beasts and monsters during the night.

If they took too long clearing away the bodies, they risked them turning into undead. And, even if that didn’t happen, leaving corpses until next spring would also risk spreading diesease.

With that in mind, both Mercurio’s army and the punitive force understood the importance of clearing away and properly disposing of the corpses. That was why, despite the tense atmosphere at times, both sides collected and disposed of all the bodies they found without any major incidents.

After Mercurio’s army and the punitive force had gathered all the bodies to a single location……

「Alright, move away.」

Rei spoke after seeing 50 corpses lined up.

Hearing his words, the soldiers quickly stepped away.

The next moment, compressed magic power enveloped Rei’s body as it turned a deep crimson.

「Phew……I’ve gotten used to activating this.」

He muttered to himself as he looked at the Fire Emperor’s Crimson Armour that covered his body.

He had managed to use the skill quite freely in battle, but activating it took quite a bit of effort.

He instinctively knew how to activate it, but he wasn’t quite how or why.

For Rei, who had basically learned the skill by using it rather than understanding how it worked, the process was a little complicated.

Of course, if he continually used it over the course of a day, he would naturally become more familiar with it.

This was one of the benefits of Fire Emperor’s Crimson Armour, the fact that it consumed less magic power than Overlord’s Armour, which ate through Rei’s magic power like crazy.

Overlord’s Armour would have been hard for Rei to keep active for several hours a day.

「Thank you.」

「Thank you for your help.」

The commanders of the units that had brought the bodies to Rei, both from Mercurio’s army and from the punitive force, bowed their heads towards him.

They couldn’t help but feel solemn as Rei cremated the bodies of their subordinates and comrades.

(Well, it would probably be better to use 『Funeral Flames』 if I could……but that uses up even more magic power than Fire Emperor’s Crimson Armour. In addition, the magic power consumption only increases if I want to cover a larger area, unlike Fire Emperor’s Crimson Armour, which I can constantly use after activating.)

Since he couldn’t use holy magic efficiently, Rei thought that it would just be better to practice using Flame Emperor’s Crimson Armour, the skill he had just learned, rather than Funeral Flames, to prevent undead from rising.

He looked over to where the bodies were lined up and detached part of the crimson flames from his armour as he threw it at them.

Deep Flame. The flames transformed into the image Rei held in his mind and the moment they came in contact with the bodies they exploded and engulfed them.

The flames were so hot that the bodies were quickly reduced to ashes.

As for what to do with the weapons, armour, and accessories that the dead bodies had on them, that was left to the people carrying the bodies.

Those who were greedy could take what they wanted.

Those who knew the person who had died might take something as a keepsake for themselves or for the deceased’s family.

Those who just mourned the dead simply carried the bodies as they were.

In this way, the Deep Flame quickly consumed the corpses.

With a battle involving tens of thousands of people like this one, there was no time for prayers after each cremation.

「Bring over the next group.」

After confirming that there were no more bodies where they had been previously lined up, Rei ordered the soldiers to bring in the next lot.

「Y-Yes!」

A frightened soldier stuttered out a response before running away.

Rei was thinking that there was no need to be that scared……but no normal soldier would want to be close to Rei while he had Fire Emperor’s Crimson Armour active.

Well, the fact that the soldier was from the punitive force likely also played a part.

As someone who had participated in the battle on the Brissa Plain and had even been part of the human wall that had surrounded the battle between Rei and Noiz, Rei’s activation of Fire Emperor’s Crimson Armour gave them nothing but nightmares.

It was only natural, considering that it was what allowed Rei to beat out Noiz, a hero of the Bestir Empire, albeit in a roundabout way.

Personally, Rei felt that victory had been given to him by Noiz as he knew that he would have still lost to Noiz in the end if they had kept fighting.

That was why Rei never considered it his victory against Noiz and was determined to win the next time they met. In order to do that, he trained to master Fire Emperor’s Crimson Armour to narrow the gap between him and Noiz, even if just a little.

「Rei-dono, they’re coming over!」

As the soldier from before called out to him, Rei could see soldiers carrying more corpses on carts.

The soldiers then proceeded to lay the bodies out on the ground in front of Rei.

As Rei watched the corpses being lined up in an orderly fashion, his eyes fell on one of them as he shouted out.

「Hey, don’t treat the corpses carelessly!」

Rei shouted out as he saw what appeared to be a mercenary throwing a corpse to the ground.

Mercenaries were people who were used to seeing death.

On a normal battlefield, corpses wouldn’t be lined up nicely to be burned like they were now. The bodies would just be tossed into a pile before burning.

Perhaps those habits were still on their minds as Rei saw the messy way in which the corpses were being handled.

For a moment, the mercenary seemed like they would complain, but they realised who who was shouting at them and continued to line the bodies up with an annoyed look on their face.

「Rei-dono, please calm down. There are all sorts of mercenaries. Some of them will properly mourn the dead and even those that don’t will handle bodies of people they know with due respect.」

The soldier from before hurriedly told Rei, as it would be no laughing matter if Rei decided to go a on rampage here.

To the soldier, Rei was the living embodiment of violence, so his reaction was only natural.

Rei was still covered by Fire Emperor’s Crimson Armour after all, so he was definitely not someone to anger at this point in time.

The mercenary still seemed a little dissatisfied, but obediently followed Rei’s orders as he knew what might happen if he didn’t.

(Who do they think I am?)

Rei thought to himself, but considering the things he had done up to now, it was hard to deny the facts.

「Hah. ……Anyhow, show more respect to the dead. Don’t treat the bodies roughly.」

At Rei’s words, the soldiers, mercenaries, and adventurers carefully laid the corpses out onto the ground.

A few minutes later, all the bodies were unloaded from the carts and everyone left.

「Rei-dono, please. This will be the last lot here, so we will move the the next location after cremating all these corpses.」

「Got it.」

Nodding at his words, tentacles grew out from the shoulders of Rei’s crimson armour as a Deep Flame flew out.

Flying through the air, the Deep Flame eventually landed at the center of the corpses before engulfing them all in flames.

After confirming that all the corpses had been cremated, he deactivated Fire Emperor’s Crimson Armour as he turned to face the soldier.

「So, where’s the next location?」

「……Ah, yes. There’s a place west of here where they’re collecting more corpses, so please go there.」

The Brissa Plain was referred to as a singular location, but it was actually a large area, enough for tens of thousands of soldiers to fight.
