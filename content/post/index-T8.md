# **Boy Saintess – Volume 1, Chapter 1**

It was a beautiful, sunny day when the royal carriage stopped at my door, and the Royal Guard surrounded the entire house, sealing it off completely.

And the owner of that royal carriage was, at this very moment, sitting across from me, sipping tea.

She lifted her teacup and took an elegant sip.

“Mm, as expected, the tea you brew is still the best, Kefei’er.”

I gave a shy smile, feeling she must be joking. How could the tea I make possibly compare to the standards of the royal palace? Even the tea leaves were a gift from her some days ago, when she mentioned a certain minister had paid tribute with a batch of high-grade black tea and decided to send some my way. Could the same tea really taste different just because I was the one who brewed it?

I forgot to introduce her. The young woman before me, dressed in a red gown with her blonde hair draped over her shoulders, is named Tristine. She is quite possibly the most graceful person I have met in my fifteen years, like a living textbook of court etiquette. Every single gesture is elegant, dignified, and flawless!

Of course, that’s only in public…

We met when she was still a princess. Hmm… you could call us childhood sweethearts, right? Though she’s a few years older than me. Today, she is the Queen, the supreme ruler of the Holy Flame Kingdom.

Oh, and when she was crowned, I was the one who placed the crown upon her head.

Of course, don’t let her appearance fool you. In private, she has a side that no one knows about.

“I’m relieved to see you’ve emerged from the shadow of the Archbishop’s passing. You were crying for quite a while a short time ago,” she said to me.

Without letting my expression change, I poured myself a cup of tea and cradled it in my hands.

“Thank you for your concern, Your Majesty. I have accepted reality.”

“Does it bother you that I’ve come to visit with such a grand entourage?”

“Not at all.”

“The main reason I came is that I have something important to tell you.”

“Hm? What is it?”

I was curious myself. Was it the evil cults in the east, the plague in the south, the demon tribe in the west, or the rebellion in the north?

I heard her say to me, “The person from the prophecy who can defeat the Demon King has appeared.”

I was stunned.

“What?! The Hero who can defeat _that person_ has appeared?”

According to the prophecy of the Holy Light, the Demon King (I’m used to calling him _that person_) will ultimately be defeated by an otherworlder from a different dimension.

And this otherworlder is the legendary figure we have all been dreaming of—the Hero.

However—

I asked her, “Ever since the prophecy of the Holy Light was revealed, didn’t _that person_ destroy all ninety-nine of the Summoning Gates we built?”

“Thanks to the grace of the Holy Light, it seems even the most careful plan can have a flaw.”

Tristine took a sip of her tea.

“One of the Summoning Gates, hidden in the palace basement, was never completely destroyed. It has been operating secretly without any of us even knowing.”

She set down her cup and leaned a little closer. I leaned forward as well, listening intently.

“Just three days ago, an otherworlder from a different dimension was truly summoned to our world!”

“You’re not joking, are you?! Is this real?” My heart pounded with excitement. “It seems the world is saved.”

“Yes, the prophecy of the Holy Light has never been wrong. The otherworlder who can turn the tide and save the kingdom has finally appeared.”

“What kind of person is the otherworlder? Is it a boy? Tall? Short? Fat or thin?” I asked curiously.

I could see Tristine hesitate for a moment.

“How should I describe him? He seems… unfathomable.”

“Unfathomable…?”

My curiosity grew even stronger. What could the otherworlder possibly be like to earn such an evaluation from the Queen?

“Are all people who transmigrate from other dimensions that incredible? Where is he now? It’s been three days, don’t tell me he’s already set off on an adventure?”

However, when I asked this, I faintly saw a trace of sorrow flash across Tristine’s exquisitely perfect face.

“Kefei’er, tell me. The otherworlder from the prophecy who can defeat the Demon King has appeared. As the Queen of the Holy Flame Kingdom, it is my duty to support him with all my power, correct?”

I nodded.

“Of course. After all, the one he has to face is _that person_.”

_That person_—the Demon King, the great archfiend!

The darkest, most evil existence in all of history! Even his name is taboo! The mere mention of him makes people turn pale with fear!

“If it’s really as the prophecy says, then no matter what method the otherworlder uses, as long as he can defeat the Demon King, I am willing to mobilize the entire kingdom’s resources to support him.”

I nodded. She was right; this had long been the consensus. As long as someone could defeat that great archfiend, no price was too high.

In the beginning, just building the ninety-nine Summoning Gates had cost an astronomical amount of resources. I was young back then, but I heard it had even evolved into a contest—for every gate _that person_ destroyed, we would build another. Countless lives were sacrificed defending them.

Now, after what felt like an eternity of waiting, the otherworlder had finally arrived. To defeat the Demon King, it was only natural to satisfy all of the otherworlder’s strategic demands.

She lowered her voice and said to me, “Even handing the army over to him wouldn’t be out of the question, but… when I explained everything to him, he asked me for…”

“For what?”

Her habit of speaking slowly and softly in public was going to be the death of me.

She gave a solemn cough.

“A villa.”

“…Huh?”

Honestly, I was completely baffled.

Do otherworlders from different dimensions not play by the rules?

“A v-villa…? Did Your Majesty grant his request?”

“I gave him the Fontainebleau Manor in the city’s outskirts. Kefei’er… the very first thing he asked for was a villa. What was I supposed to do? I felt so helpless.”

She complained to me, on the verge of tears.

Er…

This…

I wracked my brains, trying to figure out what the otherworlder could possibly be thinking.

“Actually, Your Majesty, there’s no need to feel helpless. The otherworlder has only just arrived. In order to avoid being targeted by the—Demon King—too early, choosing to hide himself might very well be part of his plan to defeat him.”

After hearing my words, her mood seemed to be soothed a little.

“Your analysis makes sense. It would be great if that were the case. Also, yesterday he dismissed all the servants at the manor.”

“What? This…”

Do otherworlders from different dimensions not play by the rules? I had the same thought again.

“He is the otherworlder in whom we have placed all our hopes, living alone in such a large manor with no one to look after him. So, I went to discuss this issue with him, and he said he only needed one maid.”

“One maid…?”

“That’s right. It would be good to have someone placed by his side. It’s just that—”

She looked at me.

“He specifically requested a beautiful, white-haired girl.”

“…Huh?”

I realized that after she spoke, she had been staring at me without blinking.

“White hair is very rare…” I said weakly.

“It is. You’re the only one I’ve ever seen,” she said, still staring at me. (>﹏<)

Her intense gaze made me break out in a cold sweat. I stammered, “Um… you don’t mean you want me to…”

“I could have just found a beautiful girl and had her dye her hair white, but Kefei’er, I want the person I place by the otherworlder’s side to be someone I trust completely. And the person I trust most is—you.”

Whoa. Wai-wai-wai-wait a minute!

I shot to my feet.

“Are you really asking me to disguise myself as a maid and go undercover by the otherworlder’s side????”

“Kefei’er, I am counting on you to supervise him and make him realize the mission he carries. I know that posing as a maid is beneath your status as the Saintess, but at this point, I can’t think of a better way…”

“N-n-n-no, that’s not it!!”

I thought to myself.

Wearing a maid outfit… that has nothing to do with my status.

The thing is…

…I’m actually a boy.

I’m getting a little stage fright. I don’t even know if I can complete this mission.

Heavens, spare me from chest pads.

Besides, my mission this time is to guide the Hero onto the right path with the ideals of the Saintess. I don’t need to rely on _certain things_ to help things along.

“Don’t worry, I promise I will complete the mission.”

Despite my embarrassment, I gave her a firm promise.

“You’ve been wronged, my dear sister.”

She hugged me and gently kissed my cheek.

I dared not imagine, if she ever found out I wasn’t her sister, but a cross-dressing pervert, would she be disgusted with me?

But she had no reason to worry, because I’m a boy!

Even if I did run into danger, as long as I revealed my identity as a boy, I’m sure that no matter how deranged the other person was, they would back off, right?

But that would expose my true identity, so it’s best to avoid that from happening.

I silently warned myself: I must hide my identity well! I absolutely cannot let it slip!

Next, accompanied by Tristine, I walked out of the bedroom, through the living room—and as the knights opened the main doors, I walked towards the carriage under the watchful eyes of the crowd…

Although as the Saintess, I’ve been used to being the center of attention at public events since I was a child, this was the first time I was being stared at while wearing a maid outfit!

Especially after knowing I’m a boy, it’s even more mortifying…

But I hope you can stay in contact with me. Just use ravens to send messages! There are a few raised at the manor.”

“Hello, I’m the maid you ordered”—something like that?

After all, if _that person_ found out the otherworlder from the Holy Light’s prophecy had appeared, he would surely rush over to kill him at the first opportunity.

Who would have thought he could see that far ahead? Compared to some hot-blooded idiot, a Hero like this impresses me much more.

“Your Majesty, Lady Saintess, we have arrived at Fontainebleau Manor.”

My sister held her sword horizontally in front of her, standing her ground. The laser beam was intercepted by her sword and split in two, streaking past us on either side! In an instant, it hit a distant spot with a deafening boom. The shockwave was so powerful it reached me, messing up my hair.

I glanced back, secretly glad that this was the barrier that person had set up. Otherwise, the entire street would have been destroyed.

“Even as a corpse… he still retains the strength he had in life?” my sister said with a frown.

I knew the sword in her hand was created by conjuration magic and had the ability to sever magic, but even so, blocking a single blow from the greatest mage of the current era was no easy feat.

Immediately after, Santa Claus closed in on us, firing laser beams from his mouth with every step. My sister swung her sword, deflecting all of them. Although the lasers weren’t as powerful as when he had gathered his magic, they still blasted deep craters into the ground.

After a barrage of attacks, it seemed the only safe place in the entire area was behind my sister. Santa Claus focused his magic into his fist and lunged forward with a punch! In the nick of time, my sister teleported away with me. We reappeared ten meters away, and the spot where we had just been standing was now a crater.

I noticed a problem. My sister was constantly on the defensive.

Looking up, Santa Claus raised both hands and sent a scorching ball of light into the sky. My sister said with slight surprise—”Even Teacher’s old spell, Unsetting Sun…?”

I looked up at the “sun” in the sky. Its brilliance scattered into countless blades of light! A torrential downpour of carpet bombing began! My sister swung her star-dusted magic robe and pulled me into her embrace.

“Fei’er, don’t move.”

“Eh…?”

In the blink of an eye, I found myself in the eye of a storm. All the attacks from the blades of light were blocked outside.

“You stay here and don’t run around. I’ll go deal with him.”

She released me and said.

“But—”

“No buts. Listen to me. I can’t let you get into danger.”

However, I saw a look of sorrow on my sister’s face. Her opponent was her former teacher. I could feel it; she couldn’t bring herself to strike her teacher, which was why she had been on the defensive the whole time.

I watched her back disappear into the storm wall. No…! I can’t stay here. Right now, I’m the only one who can help my sister…!

This time, I didn’t listen to her. Whoa… she’s probably going to scold me for this later, right? But, but, I really want to help.

I walked out of the eye of the storm. As far as I could see, my sister was locked in a fierce battle with Santa Claus, wielding her black Blade of Nothingness.

Pure white Holy Light condensed into a hammer in my hand. The head of the hammer was cylindrical, and the thin handle was taller than I was.

I held the hammer upside down, watching from a distance. I was originally looking for a good opportunity, but Sister Ejielia immediately noticed me, a look of shock on her face!

“Fei’er, you???”

Oh no—! I had distracted my sister. Santa Claus knocked her away with a punch. Apparently, he noticed me too!? The reindeer pulling the sleigh descended from the sky, and Santa Claus jumped onto it! He flew straight towards me!

So fast—!

I was startled and couldn’t help but tighten my grip on the hammer. I saw a huge silver spear materialize in Santa Claus’s hand! I-I… I don’t know if this is his ultimate move or what, but he’s coming at me!!!

At this point, I… had to go all out! The moment we clashed, I swung the hammer! I smashed it right into Santa Claus’s face, breaking through his magic defense and knocking him off the sleigh—!

I twirled the hammer like a pen, watching as Santa Claus tumbled on the ground, rolling for more than ten meters before stopping, like a broken puppet, completely destroyed.

Seriously… I had no other choice but to help Sister Ejielia and let her teacher find true peace…

# **48 – Epilogue**

The pure white, long-handled hammer spun in my hand as easily as a pen. After a few twirls, I let it rest. With that one strike just now, my sister’s teacher was finally at peace.

Phew… that was a close call, wasn’t it?

“Fei’er…!”

“…!?”

I met Sister Ejielia’s gaze. Her eyes were dull and lifeless; she looked like she was about to snap.

“I-I’m sorry…! I shouldn’t have disobeyed you and run out on my own…”

Whoa… I completely gave myself away. She approached me in just a few steps, and I quickly hid the “weapon”—the hammer—behind my back. “I… I only acted because I saw you couldn’t bring yourself to strike your teacher… I’m so sorry for taking matters into my own hands…!”

Speaking of which… after I smashed my sister’s teacher with a single blow, there was probably… no putting him back together, right?

I braced myself for a scolding, but instead, she cupped my cheeks with both hands, her eyes welling with tears.

“You, you, you… are you hurt anywhere…?”

I froze for a moment, a little overwhelmed. She looked me up and down, frantically searching for wounds that didn’t exist. I saw her tears fall to the ground.

My sister had never cried in front of me before.

“N-No… I’m really not hurt…!” I said quickly.

“That was too reckless! Do you have any idea? That silver spear just now was our teacher’s ultimate move, the Hunt of Light!”

Her face was grim, her hands trembling.

“I’m sorry… maybe my hammer was a little longer, so I hit him first?” I said weakly.

“It’s my fault. I… I could never get past the fact that it was our teacher, and I nearly put you in danger! What would I have done if something had happened to you…”

As if all her strength had left her, my sister collapsed to the ground, hugging me and crying.

The hammer disintegrated into specks of light and vanished. I knelt down with her and patted her head to comfort her. After all, I’m a boy. In times like these, of course I should be the one comforting my sister.

“It’s okay now, it’s all over…”

For the moment, everything was over. _That person_ was gone, my sister’s teacher was finally at peace, and as the barrier disappeared, we were back on the streets of the royal capital.

It was just that I didn’t know when _that person_ would suddenly reappear to make her presence known, hanging over me and Sister Ejielia like a nightmare that couldn’t be shaken off. But I had to be strong. I’m a boy. If even I started crying and needed my sister to comfort me, that would be pathetic.

“Fei’er? You two…”

—It was Lian Qi’s voice!? Thank goodness! She was safe and sound. It was a great blessing that _that person_ hadn’t discovered her existence. I looked over and saw her staring dumbfounded at my sister and me, huddled together.

“What happened? Everyone was running for their lives. I got pushed around by the crowd and lost my sense of direction. When I looked back, you were gone. I could only stay here and wait for you.”

“The Demon King… came.”

“The Demon King…?”

“I was attacked by the Demon King. Luckily, Sister Ejielia saved me…”

“What…?”

Lian Qi looked astonished. Then, she seemed to notice the third person lying ten meters away—

“Hey? Isn’t that Santa Claus? How did he get broken?”

“That’s my teacher!” my sister shouted, her face grim. “The Demon King dressed his corpse like that just to torment me!”

Sister Ejielia stood up, her face stern, and walked straight to Lian Qi. I didn’t know what she was planning to do—without a word, she grabbed Lian Qi’s shoulders and started shaking her violently, as if Lian Qi were living in a dream that she desperately needed to be shaken out of.

“You’re an otherworlder too, right!? The child of prophecy! Please, I’m begging you, defeat the Demon King! Don’t let him torment us anymore!”

“Sister… don’t! You’ll scare… Lian Qi.” I quickly tried to stop her. I could understand how my sister felt, but… forcing Lian Qi wouldn’t do any good.

The atmosphere instantly quieted.

“I’m sorry, I lost my composure.”

My sister released Lian Qi, turning her back to her with a sigh.

“Forcing you is useless. Please excuse me, I’ll be back in a moment.”

Was she going off to cry in private? I was about to call out to her, but she vanished in a gust of wind. The crimson moon hung high in the sky, Santa Claus’s corpse lay on the street below, and only Lian Qi and I were left.

Lian Qi looked at me silently for a moment and then said, “Are… are you hurt?”

“I’m fine, don’t worry about me. And please don’t blame Sister Ejielia, she was just very upset.”

“I’m not blaming her,” she said softly.

I secretly breathed a sigh of relief. “That’s good… I hope this didn’t put too much pressure on you.”

Lian Qi was silent for a moment, then asked, “Did she just say the Demon King is also an otherworlder?”

“Yes, that’s why the Demon King is so concerned about the prophecy. He even destroyed the ninety-nine Summoning Gates we built, because he’s an otherworlder from a different dimension himself.”

“I see. So that’s how it is.”

Lian Qi nodded thoughtfully, kicking a loose stone on the ground.

“The Demon King must think he’s the protagonist since he crossed over to another world, so he sees me, who also has a protagonist’s halo, as a thorn in his side, right?”

“So… I feel sorry for you too… After all, your fated rival is _that person_…”

It would be perfectly normal if Lian Qi decided to quit and find a way back to her own world, right? After all, there’s no need to risk your life for a strange world…

“Is it… too late for me to start learning magic now?”

Eh eh eh eh???

Lian Qi suddenly blurted that out, and I stared at her in shock!

Does her asking that question mean…?! She’s planning to stay and fight?

“It’s not too late!”

I was overjoyed and answered her immediately. I couldn’t have been more excited!

“The Demon King also started out as a student! So, if it’s you, Lian Qi, there’s definitely no problem!”

“You have that much faith in me…”

She scratched her head.

“Actually, it’s because I saw you get attacked by the Demon King. It made me feel protective of you. I just want to protect you.”

Protective…

As a boy, I felt quite ashamed.

But her words made me feel a sense of clarity.

I’ve made up my mind! And so, with my hands behind my back, I gave her a smile—”Then let me cheer you on!”

“You’ll really stay with me?”

“Mhm!”

I nodded.

“Until you’ve grown strong enough to defeat the Demon King, I’ll be the one to protect you…!”
