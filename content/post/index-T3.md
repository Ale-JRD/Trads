# 815

The final and likely most intense battle in the Bestir Empire’s civil war.

Gurgast swung both his battleaxes on the front lines, slicing through any punitive force soldiers that stood in his way.

Despite his age, he showed no signs of fatigue as he rampaged through the enemy with the force of a storm.

「Hey hey! What’s wrong, what’s wrong!? Aren’t you the punitive force!? How can you not deal with a single old man like me!」

As he shouted, Gurgast cut through another soldiers with his battleaxe, leather armour and all.

His cuts weren’t clean like Rei’s attacks, rather Gurgasts attacks would literally chop the enemy soldiers to pieces.

「General, general! Saying thing like ‘I’m an old man’ isn’t going to convince anyone who sees you right now!」

One of Gurgast’s subordinates responded as they brandished their own halberd.

「Gahahahahahah, you’re absolutely right! If the general is an old man, then I wonder how many real monsters there truly are in the world!」

Another of Gurgast’s subordinates, who had been listening in, laughed out loud as their curved sword sliced off a soldier’s head.

An overwhelming slaughter was unfolding around them during this time.

……However, unlike most situations, the slaughter was being inflicted on the side with more soldiers.

The punitive force had the superior numbers and were also coordinating with each other properly.

And yet they were still on the losing end of the exchange.

They weren’t just being slowly pushed back either, they were being one-sidedly crushed.

It was as if they had become the prey in a hunt.

「Why, why are we getting pushed back so hard!? They’re not even fighting together, they’re just doing whatever they want……so why!?」

「That’s because you lot are just too weak!」

Gurgast cut the screaming soldier in half with a single strike from his right battleaxe.

Despite the soldier being a citizen of the Bestir Empire, Gurgast showed no hesitation.

His mouth even curved up slightly as he looked around and saw his subordinates enjoying the battle in their own way.

At first glace he appeared to be more like a bandit leader, and that wasn’t entirely wrong.

Gurgast’s unit was made up of soldiers who had never considered fighting together with others, preferring to fight their own battles.

The minimum level of restraint they had was to not attack those who surrendered and become prisoners.

……As for the punitive force soldiers who tried to take advantage of that to pretend to surrender before secretly attacking, they were quickly killed with their heads or bodies split apart.

「Hey hey, put your all into this! Your all! I don’t need anyone who has their spirit beaten back by their enemies!」

With a shout……or rather a shout better described as a roar, Gurgast’s battleaxe swung out again, knocking aside the long sword which a soldier tried to use to block before his second battleaxe crushed their head.

「Boss, we’ve split from Vihera-sama and the others. Should we wait?」

「It doesn’t matter. Vihera will meet back up with us soon. ……And more importantly, don’t call me boss! I’m not a bandit!」

Perhaps due to his excitement from the battle, Gurgast spoke Vihera’s name without any titles, despite being a former retainer.

One strike deflected the tip of a cavalry’s lance while a second strike cut through the horse’s leg.

As the rider tried to regain their balance after falling to the ground, Gurgast simply crushed their neck.

「Hmph, there’s no way for a cavalry charge to be effective in such a messy environment.」

He spat out as he looked for his next prey.

「I would like to stop you there. I wouldn’t be able to apologise to Prince Kabajid if we were to lose any more of our forces.」

A figure appeared as they spoke.

His body was covered in leather armour that didn’t restrict his movement.

The leather armour wasn’t made from ordinary monsters, but from a high ranking monster’s hide.

The sword in their hand wasn’t an ordinary long sword, but a magic sword.

It was clear that they weren’t just an ordinary soldier but a well known person.

The figure seemed familiar to Gurgast, who thought for a moment……before recognising who it was.

「Was your name, Bratta?」

As a skilled fighter among the First Prince’s faction, Bratta was well known for his confidence in battle.

The main reason why Gurgast hadn’t recognised him immediately was because he had mostly spent his time holed up in his own territory after Vihera ran away from the Bestir Empire.

As an eccentric person himself, Gurgast hadn’t gone to the Imperial Capital since.

He simply sought battle within his own territory.

……As a side effect of this, Gurgast’s Obrisin County became the target of bandits hoping to test their skills for a while, before eventually becoming one of the safest territories in the Bestir EMpire.

Gurgast himself, while a battle maniac, didn’t enjoy challenging weaker opponents to battle. He wasn’t someone fond of luxury either, so the taxes in his territory were comparatively lower than the territories of other nobles.

What his love of combat did result in was an increase in blacksmiths and shops who dealt in weapons and armour, which all received a fair amount of subsidies for them to do business.

This all led to a territory that was comfortable to live in and for people to gather, which was why Mercurio and Theorem had made it their original base of operations for the civil war.

「Yes, I know your name without you even needing to say it. You’re famous after all. ……But, just because you’re famous doesn’t mean you can go around decimating our forces, that would be a problem.」

Bratta pointed the tip of his sword towards Gurgast.

At the same time, a thin layer of water started to cover the blade.

「A magic sword that can control water, huh? Troublesome, but……that is all it is!」

No wanting to waste any more time, Gurgast charged forward with both battleaxes in hand.

「UOOOOOOH-!」

With a roar, Gurgast swung the battleaxe in his right hand.

Bratta blocked the attack with his magic sword as the water where the weapons clashed slid past Gurgast’s weapon and towards his body……

「Fuu-!」

But, before the water could reach him, Gurgast swung his arm out with a determined shout, scattering the water.

「Tch-, well done!」

「You think you can do anything to me with such a sloppy attack!?」

Gurgast swung his right and left battleaxes, one after the other, slightly staggering their timings to make them harder to avoid.

While Bratta had a magic sword, it wasn’t at the level where he could clash against a battleaxe head on……let alone continuously.

In fact, maybe due to its ability to control water, his magic sword was a lot weaker compared to other magic swords.

Of course, as a magic sword, it was still sturdier then ordinary long swords.

「Take this!」

With a shout, Bratta thrust out his sword.

His attack was so fast that all Gurgast could do was to try to deflect it with his axes.

However……that moment of deflection was what Bratta was aiming for.

「Just as I predicted!」

Water gathered at the tip of his magic sword, forming into a ball.

With the ball of water gathered at it’s tip, Bratta’s weapon would be better described as a mace made from water rather than a sword.

Reaching the conclusion that his long sword, which focused on speed and skill, was at a disadvantage against dual battleaxes, Bratta decided to use the mass of water as a weapon itself.

His weapon, or rather mass on a stick at this point, considering the 1m diameter ball of water at the end of his sword, swung down towards Gurgast.

A ball of water that big would normally be quite heavy and hard to swing.

However, Bratta’s magic sword had the ability to control water.

That was why he was able to still swing his sword around without any difficulty.

If his attack had hit, even a seasoned warrior like Gurgast would have taken significant injury.

……Yes. If it had hit.

「You think such basic attack will work on me!?」

Gurgast roared angrily as he swung both his battleaxes.

His attacks, made with impeccable speed and timing, destroyed the ball of water as it swung towards him……and at that moment, Gurgast saw a smile form on Bratta’s lips.

Unfortunately for Bratta, while Gurgast could see his smile, he failed to see the smile on Gurgast’s face.

Both of them considered this exchange of attacks to merely be a warm up before the real attack, but whether both sides knew this or not had minimal impact on the final outcome.

「HAAAAAAAAAAH-!」

「UOOOOOOOOOOH-!」

Both men gave shouts filled with fighting spirit as they unleashed their finishing blows……

「……I was…….was……overwhelmed? I abandoned my friends and fled pathetically when facing Crimson……I got this weapon to become much stronger……」

「You lost because you sought strength in other things. Know that your strength is yours alone.」

Gurgast muttered as both his battleaxes cut into Bratta’s left and right shoulders.

As for Bratta’s magic sword, it stopped just short of reaching Gurgast’s neck.

It was just an instant……just a split second, but that difference was enough to decided the battle.

「Da……mn……it……」

Reaching his limit, Bratta bare got those words out of his mouth before collapsing to the ground.

Gurgast gave a snort as he watched Bratta’s magic sword fall to the ground before speaking.

「Hmph, that’s because you relied too much on your magic sword rather than improving your own strength. ……Hey, take this guy to the rear and treat him. I’m taking him prisoner.」

「Oh, boss. You didn’t kill him?」

「He has a lot of weaknesses, but he was still quite impressive. ……Also, stop calling me ‘boss’.」

Putting down his right battleaxe, Gurgast took out some potions from a pouch he had before pouring it randomly over the unconscious Bratta.

「It’s rare for you to rate someone so highly, boss.」

「It’s just your imagination. He’s got some talent. With some more experience, he’ll be able to go toe-to-toe with me. ……Also, I told you to stop calling me ‘boss’.」

Gurgast replied with a slightly annoyed expression, but his subordinate didn’t seem to care.

「I understand. I’ll have him sent to the rear right away boss!」

「……Ah, whatever. Just take him away.」

Perhaps realising there was no point scolding his subordinate any further, Gurgast gave a sigh before picking his battleaxe back up.

His subordinate picked up Bratta at the same time before swiftly leaving.

「Seriously. ……But, well……that was quite the good fight.」

Though still a little annoyed that his subordinate continued to call him ‘boss’, a fierce smile formed on Gurgast’s lips as he thought about the first fulfilling fight he’d had in a while.

Looking around, the battle was still raging and he gripped his battleaxes tighter in his excitement.

「Now then……you’ll have to fight with me a bit longer to calm this excitement!」

With that shout, Gurgast charged towards the nearest punitive force soldier, slashing at them.

The soldier, who had been trying to sneak attack some of Gurgast’s subordinates, who were fighting nearby, instinctively thrust their spear out due to their overwhelming fear of death.

In terms of taking advantage of an enemy’s defenses, spears were a good weapon, considering their range and ability to be thrust out.

But……against someone wielding battleaxes in both hands and with a wicked smile on their lips, a spear was no match.

No, rather, a more skilled soldier might have been able to do something.

However, they wouldn’t still be a soldier at that point and they probably wouldn’t have been able to handle Gurgast in the end either.

「You dare to do something as dirty as sneak attacking us!?」

With an angry shout, Gurgast’s battleaxes split the soldier diagonally in half as the strong smell of blood spread out.

……Naturally, the strong smell of blood was only to be expected, considering how fierce the fighting was on this battlefield.

With Bratta, one of the punitive force’s strongest fighters, defeated and taken prisoner, the battle started to swing heavily in Mercurio’s favour.

# 816

By the time Vihera arrived along with her subordinates, including Tilleyle, the battle was already nearing its end.

Gurgast stood at the center, his eyes narrowed in satisfaction as he looked around.

Vihera, who was a fellow battle maniac, and Tilleyle, who had known Gurgast for a long time, knew that he wasn’t surveying his surroundings, but rather recalling the battle that had just occurred.

A strong smell of blood hung in the air from the corpses of the punitive force soldiers as well as several of Gurgast’s subordinates which were scattered everywhere, but Vihera didn’t seem to care as she went over to Gurgast.

As a battle maniac herself, Vihera was no stranger to corpses.

She didn’t particularly like seeing them, but she wasn’t going to scream at the sight of them like an ignorant person either.

「Gurgast, you seem to be in a good mood. Did the battle satisfy you?」

「……Vihera-sama? Well, it wasn’t a fight I was completely satisfied with, but it was still quite enjoyable. It was a far cry from when I fought Rei though.」

Gurgast spoke as he looked at both his battleaxes with an ecstatic gaze.

It was a look that would normally stop anyone from getting close to him.

His aura at this time just didn’t feel right.

However, Vihera just nodded with a small smile at his words.

「I see, that’s good.」

「……It seems you had an enjoyable battle on your side as well?」

Understanding the meaning of Gurgast’s gaze, Vihera smiled ambiguously as she spoke.

「I don’t know about that. They were certainly strong, but their style of combat was a complete mismatch to mine. I had the overwhelming advantage in the match up. If you asked me whether it was fun or not though……well, I guess I would say it was fun.」

Vihera’s response was vague as she thought of Digma.

As an A rank adventurer with a title, Digma certainly lived up to his name.

But as his title of Water Dragon suggested, Digma was more suited to fighting large numbers of opponents.

Of course his combat skills were by no means weak and could even be considered very strong if he fought alongside his water dragon.

Still, Vihera hadn’t been able to lose herself in the excitement of combat as much as she had hoped.

Gurgast was a little suspicious at Vihera’s reply, but Tilleyle interjected before he could say anything.

「Now then, since we’ve cleared this area, shall we keep advancing? Rei has already caused quite a lot of damage to their rear guard. Surely it wouldn’t be a problem to charge straight for their main camp?」

「……That’s right. Gurgast, you……no, looking at you, there’s no need to ask.」

As Vihera looked over at Gurgast, he swung both battleaxes in his hands to show that he still had the strength to fight……no, more than enough strength to fight.

It had been some time since Vihera had fought with Digma and her opponents since then hadn’t posed much of a challenge, so she had been able to recover a fair amount of stamina.

Tilleyle had yet to need to fight in person, giving orders to his subordinates instead, so he was still doing fine physically.

Gurgast’s subordinates had expended a lot of their stamina in combat, but with the adrenaline rush they were all having, they would probably be able to continue fighting for little longer, if not a lot longer.

The biggest source of concern at this point were the soldiers under Tilleyle’s command.

Not that they were a hindrance in any way of course.

On the contrary, by most standards, they could be considered elites.

It was just that everyone else present was so abnormal that Tilleyle’s unit were the only ones where it might be a concern.

「Tilleyle.」

With just one word, Vihera’s subordinates knew what she wanted to say. Tilleyle gave a deep bow towards Vihera.

「Leave it to me, Vihera-sama. My soldiers and I will ensure that your exploits on the battlefield will be glorious.」

「……Fufu, same as always. But please don’t push yourself too hard.」

Vihera spoke to Tilleyle, who appeared the same despite all this time on the battlefield, with a charming smile.

Tilleyle spread his arms wide as if overcome by emotion at Vihera’s smile.

「Let us go now! To put an end to this battle……this civil war, that has pitted brothers against each other. And to show the greatness of Vihera-sama!」

「Seriously, he never changes, no matter how much time passes by.」

Gurgast sighed, but his whole body seemed radiate a joy for combat as he continued onward.

Seeing that, Vihera also stepped forth, looking forward to the end of this civil war.

Vihera stood at the center with Gurgast behind her to the right and Tilleyle behind her to the left.

Following behind them were their soldiers.

The central battle lines had already completely collapsed and the few soldiers who had remained had been swiftly dispatched by Gurgast and his subordinates.

Any who were still left were in no state to lay a hand on Vihera as she advanced through the battlefield.

In this situation, some even found Vihera’s confident and fearless figure on the battlefield to be like the descent of a deity.

As Vihera and her subordinates advanced through the deserted battlefield, only those who remained loyal to their masters didn’t flee.

Those remaining soldiers somehow managed to rally a final defense, waiting at the end of the battlefield before the rear guard.

「……Do it!」

「He we come.」

With just those words, the final fight of the central battlefront began.

In terms of numbers, the punitive force still had the advantage.

Even after being at a disadvantage for so long, the fact that they still had the superior numbers spoke to the punitive force’s true strength.

Of course, that strength was also the Bestir Empire’s strength.

Vihera honestly wanted to avoid killing as many of them as possible.

They knew that the battle was already lost, and yet they still remained loyal to the very end.

Of course, not all of them were truly loyal.

Some still hoped that they would be able to pull off a miracle reversal, earning themselves a large reward, while others were still being lured in by Vihera’s beauty.

Despite being such a mixed bunch, they still hoped to turn the battle around at this point and were confident enough in their own ability to face Vihera and her subordinates head on.

Vihera’s main combat power at this point was still in Gurgast and his soldiers, with Tilleyle’s soldiers having tired out from the long battle.

Naturally, Gurgast and his soldier remained the main combat force while Tilleyle’s soldiers fell back to their usual supporting role.

As for the punitive force soldiers that remained, they were a mixed group with no real coordination between them.

Because of that, after clashing head on……

「You dare to challenge me with such meager skills? Try again when you’re stronger.」

Vihera muttered to herself as she knocked out another soldier using Magic Palm while dodging a spear thrust.

Looking around, the fight could only be described as a massacre.

It was a fight that the punitive force stood no chance of winning from the beginning.

All they had been able to do was to buy enough time for the rear guard to regroup.

And above all, as they believed, time for Kabajid and Shurus to come up with a countermeasure.

「Damn it! Don’t retreat! We cannot retreat! If we lose here, all that’s left is the rear guard! We have to hold out! Until Prince Shurus comes up with a plan!」

One of the soldiers facing Vihera let out a heartfelt shout.

『UOOOOOOOOH-!』

Those words seemed to galvanise the remaining soldiers.

They swung their weapons desperately, hoping to reduce the enemy by even a single person.

「Die-!」

「Oh, you’ve got a good eye, attacking me. I’m definitely weaker than Vihera-sama and Gurgast.」

Tilleyle twisted his body as he spoke, dodging the spear thrust targeted at his body.

「But……」

With those words, Tilleyle quickly drew his rapier from the sheath at his waist before slicing off the tip of the spear.

A rapier was a weapons mostly known for its thrusting attacks.

So how had Tilleyle cut off the spear tip so easily? It was simply because he was using a magic rapier.

Of course, it wasn’t a particularly special magic rapier.

It couldn’t generate any of the elements, like fire, wind, lighting, water, ice, or earth, like typical magic weapons, and it was only slightly more durable compared to an ordinary rapier.

What made this rapier a magic weapon was the effect it had that allowed a slashing effect to occur wherever its tip was thrust towards.

「Eh?」

The soldier with the spear let out a confused voice, seemingly unable to understand how the tip of his weapon had been cut off.

「I also serve Vihera-sama. Naturally, I have to posses at least this level of skill.」

Tilleyle swiftly thrust his rapier out as he spoke.

Two thrusts in under a second.

By Vihera or Gurgast’s standards, it was nothing fancy, but for the average soldier, it was still too fast to track and avoid with their own eyes.

The thrusts pierced through the gaps in the soldier’s armour that covered his elbow and torso, applying its magic effect immediately as it tore through skin and ripped through flesh.

It wasn’t quite enough to cut through bone, which was why it was still a low grade magic weapon.

Of course, even low grade magic weapons weren’t commonly available.

The only reason the Bestir Empire even possessed such low grade magic weapons was thanks to their advances in alchemy.

「AHHHHHHHH-!」

But, even if it didn’t cut through the bone, tearing through skin and flesh was still extremely painful.

If the soldier hadn’t noticed the cuts, they might not have felt the pain immediately due to the excitement from combat. However, seeing the injuries on their arm and body made it hard to not notice.

They weren’t sharp slashes either, but proper cuts.

As the soldier knelt on the ground, screaming in pain, Tilleyle knocked them out before searching for his next opponent.

Unfortunately for the soldier, they had assumed Tilleyle to be a weak and easy target. Fortunately for them on the other hand, it was Tilleyle they were fighting.

Tilleyle understood Vihera’s desire to kill as few people as possible since the battle had already been decided and knocked out those he could.

Of course, Vihera’s wish to leave as many soldier alive didn’t come from an aversion to killing but a desire to maintain the Bestir Empire’s strength after the war. That said……

「AAAAAHHHHH-!」

A soldier screamed in agony as their right arm was severed by a single battleaxe.

It was Gurgast who had made the attack.

Gurgast didn’t care for either Vihera or Tilleyle’s thoughts as he merciless swung his battleaxes at anyone who stood in his way.

If there had been anyone with Bratta’s strength or qualities, he might have left them alive, but to Gurgast, any enemies he could describe as weaklings would be killed without question.

「Damn it! Stop them! Stop them here! The rear guard is still reorganising! If we let them through, they’ll collapse in an instant and will be able to push all the way to the main camp!」

The highest ranking commander among the surviving punitive force shouted out.

They had to buy more time, which was why they desperately doing whatever they could to raise morale, even if just a little……

「Oh, really? That is really good to know.」

As the commander turned to face the voice……he was distracted by the sight of a sensual woman, unsuited for a place like the battlefield.

He felt something touch his armour, but never found out what it was.

The sight of their highest ranking commander being knocked out only served to further demoralise the punitive force.

They were now at the point where they were outnumbered and outmatched, only holding out through what little morale that remained……and with that gone, the soldiers’ final defensive line crumbled in an instant.

Like so, Vihera’s soldiers, joined by the rest of Mercurio’s army that had moved up since, advanced towards the punitive force’s rear guard.

# 817

Vihera showed an expression with a hint of surprise upon seeing the rear guard in formation.

It was hard to believe that they had been completely overrun by Rei and Set just earlier.

The way their formations were tightly organised seemed to indicate that they were determined to stop anyone from passing through. But……

「It seems only a small number of them managed to reform ranks.」

Tilleyle muttered out loud from his position to the side behind Vihera. Vihera looked further back behind the rear guard’s front line to confirm the truth of his words.

There, she could see that not all the rear guard had managed to reform ranks and that there was still noticeable confusion.

Gurgast seemed to see the same thing and snorted as he held his battle axes.

「The rear guard was originally a force intended to protect the rear and provide support for the front lines. Of course, they’re not as good as the soldiers on the front lines. Naturally, there are cases where the rear is actually an elite force waiting to deliver a fatal strike against the enemy……but that’s not the case here.」

As a battle maniac, Gurgast had a strong sense for battle. Vihera smiled at Gurgast’s words.

「It is quite unfortunate that there are no strong enemies left to fight, but……this battle has been going for some time now. Having been pushed this far back, I doubt the punitive force has any options left.」

Vihera didn’t say it out loud out of consideration for Gurgast, but Rei had already defeated Noiz, the punitive force’s strongest fighter, and she had taken Digma prisoner, their second strongest fighter.

Of course, the punitive force still had many more strong fighters, but they were spread across the battlefield, so with Set and Rei on the right and left wings respectively, it would be difficult for any of them to make it back any time soon.

(Gurgast would probably start sulking if he found out that Noiz had been on the battlefield.)

Gurgast’s desire for combat made Noiz, an S rank adventurer, a target to reach.

If Vihera told him that Noiz had been defeated by Rei, it would probably cause Gurgast to sulk, even if he didn’t show it.

Gurgast, with all his tough looks, sulking.

Those who knew him superficially might have found it hard to believe, but those who knew him well, like Vihera and Tilleyle, could tell.

……Although it was still hard to notice considering how rare of an expression it was for him.

「Now then……if we can just make it through the rear guard, victory will be ours. Are you ready? We’ve come this far, so don’t do anything stupid and get yourself killed, okay? If you do, I’ll laugh at you after the battle.」

Vihera’s teasing words made everyone smile.

They knew that they were still at a disadvantage in turns of numbers, so the fact that they were able to laugh at the joke spoke to their strength of will.

They were all absolutely determined not to die here.

On the other side, the punitive force’s rear guard also knew that the battle would be lost if the enemy made it to their main camp, so they desperately tried to muster all the fighting spirit they could.

But ultimately, the rear guard’s job was to support the vanguard.

In terms of strength, they were fundamentally weaker……

「We outnumber them! In battle, with greater numbers comes victory! We cannot lose here! We are the last shield of Prince Kabajid and Prince Shurus! As long as we can hold out here, reinforcements will arrive from both wings! I’m not ordering you to win. All we need to do is to hold out long enough!」

One noble leading part of the rear guard shouted out in an attempt to boost the morale of their soldiers.

However, a number of soldiers were unaffected, having no faith in the noble’s shouts.

It was a matter of course. They had only just been brutality beaten by someone who was the embodiment of quality over quantity.

Many of them had witnessed their fellow soldiers blasted away by Rei, limbs broken and bodies covered in horrific burns from his Fire Emperor’s Crimson Armour.

For those who had seen that, the truth was heavier than a hundred lies and the battle was now hopeless to them.

But, despite that, the noble continued to shout.

Even if no one believed his words, they couldn’t retreat here……and they couldn’t be broken through either.

(Damn it, if only the whole rear guard was able to fight, we might have been able to do something……)

The noble spat in disgust as he thought that.

Rei’s breach of the rear guard, along with his Griffon, had caused incalculable losses to the rear guard.

(Why can a Griffon breathe fire? Why can it use water, ice, and wind magic? What kind of Griffon is that!?)

The damage had already been great, but what made it worse was the confusion caused by Set using skills that an ordinary Griffon would never use.

As a result, word started to spread among the soldiers that Set was a rare species Griffon, with rare species of monsters typically placed one rank higher than their base species.

In other words, that would mean Set was an S rank monster, further increasing the confusion.

Considering this, the fact that the noble had been able to reorganise his own soldiers at all meant that he was already quite capable.

Even if his soldiers were barely functional as an army.

「Hii- Hiiii-!」

Gurgast’s fierce expression as he charged forward with a fighting spirit filled roar was enough to crush the spirits of the punitive force’s soldiers.

His ferocious appearance even caused several soldiers to gasp in fear.

Seeing that, the noble realised that they would be overwhelmed if nothing changed.

This was just the start, they hadn’t even crossed swords with the enemy and they were already ready to run away.

Cowardice was also contagious.

(If I want to stop it……damn it!)

The battle lines would rapidly collapse if the soldiers started to fear their enemy.

The noble could only think of one thing to do to stop it.

「Don’t retreat! If we retreat here, we’ll be defeated! If you don’t want to be defeated, you have to hold out! Reinforcements will arrive soon!」

「Shut up! Why don’t you do something about it yourself!」

The soldier’s words were certainly not the kind of language one would usually use towards a noble.

It was something that would only happen on the battlefield, but the noble didn’t reprimand the soldier……rather, he shouted out as if they were waiting for someone to say that.

「Very well! Then I’ll go myself! I will defeat Count Obrisin!」

With a shout, the noble lightly kicked the sides of his horse, which started galloping at full speed.

That was the plan he had come up with, he would go to the front and fight Gurgast himself. Even if he couldn’t win, he could still fight on equal terms.

Just doing that would be enough to raise the morale of his soldiers.

He did this because he was the strongest fight present, although that was only relative to the soldiers around him.

Among nobles who had received specialised combat training, high ranking adventurers, or veteran mercenaries……he would have been average at best.

Still, to prevent his soldiers from falling apart completely, it was his only option.

「HAAAAAH-!」

With a rousing shout, the noble readied his spear as he charged towards Gurgast.

He was outmatched in skill, so he couldn’t win in a direct fight.

That was why, he would land the first strike with the help of his horse.

The noble rode forward with that plan in mind……

「Hmph.」

Gurgast gave a small smile as he watched the noble charging towards him on horseback.

Their spear was longer than what ordinary soldiers had and was clearly a high quality weapon.

A spear like that would easily be able to pierce his armour.

But……

「AAAAHHHH-!」

Gurgast let out a roar the moment their weapons clashed as he swung his battle axes.

His left battle axe was the first to be swung out.

「……That doesn’t mean you were completely useless though, right? From what Theorem told me, you still took out a number of enemies. Not just soldiers, but commanders, knights, and nobles as well.」

Rei asked again, more for confirmation of his guesses than to encourage him, but Pelnyx shook his head helplessly.

「As elite soldiers, we’re not satisfied with such small victories. ……Not just because of the expectations of others, but our own expectations. That’s why the guerrilla unit feels ashamed that we didn’t manage to do what we were supposed to do. ……I would like to hear Captain Rei speak to them about that.」

Nodding back at Pelnyx, Rei continued towards the area the guerrilla unit was within the medical clinic.

They hadn’t received typical injuries, it had been something more akin to a curse.

That was why, just to be safe, they were grouped into a separate area away from the other injured.

While they were likely fine at this point, there was always the possibility that something like that could recur again.

The Wyvern leather armour they had been wearing had been removed, but they still wanted to be sure.

Rei went over to them, seemingly without any concern, as he called out.

「You guys okay? I heard you went through a lot of trouble.」

At first, the injured didn’t seem to realise who was calling out to them.

They looked stunned, then panicked, as they looked around.

「Captain Rei! If you come over now……」

「That’s right! What if there’s a chance some bad effect might still be lingering!?」

Hearing those words, which carried more concern for Rei than themselves, Rei smiled as he spoke again.

「Don’t worry about it. You know how strong I am, right? Even if you guys are still suffering from some aftereffect, it’s highly unlikely that it will have any effect on me.」

「Yes, but we might go out of control again……」

As one of them started to speak, he noticed Rei looking at him with a smile and stopped himself.

However……it was already too late and Rei grinned as he responded.

「Hm? You think your rampage is enough to put me in danger? I didn’t think you were that good. Okay, now that the civil war is over and things have settled down, let’s test our skills tomorrow. That shouldn’t be a problem, right?」

「There definitely is!」

The man who had just spoken immediately replied without any hesitation.

Rei shook his head with a different smile from the one he had just before.

「Nope. I’ve already decided.」

「Uwah, why does Captain Rei have the brightest smile on his face!?」

「You idiot, you idiot! You idiot! You idiot!」

The others all yelled at the one who had put them in this situation.

Meanwhile, Rei took out his prized Gamelion meat skewers from the Misty Ring.

The Gamelions which the meat came from had been killed and cooked last year, but as time didn’t pass inside the Misty Ring, the Skewers were still freshly grilled.

「Here, have this for now and cheer up. I need you all to get back to being the usual guerrilla unit.」

「Captain……I’m glad to hear you say that, but now that the civil war is over, our unit will be disbanded.」

That was right, the guerrilla unit had been formed by gathering the best soldiers, adventurers, and mercenaries in Mercurio’s army into a single unit, they were a mixed bunch to put it bluntly.

Naturally, they had only been grouped together because of the civil war, so now that it was over, their unit would naturally be disbanded.

「That’s true. The guerrilla unit will definitely be disbanded. However……only after the victory parade.」

「……Victory parade?」

「Yes. Apparently it’s necessary to tell the entire Bestir Empire that the civil war is over. ……Well, not just the Bestir Empire. I heard that the neighbouring countries are also very interested in the result of this civil war. I think it’s mostly to show off to them.」

「But this parade, can we join it?」

As one of them asked Rei nervously, Rei nodded back.

「Our guerrilla unit is one of the most elite units in Mercurio’s army. With that said, you absolutely need to be in the parade. Besides……in this battle, as your commanding officer, I was doing completely different things from you. So it wouldn’t be a bad idea for us to celebrate together at the very end at least.」

「Captain Rei……you’re not going to ask Prince Mercurio for this, are you?」

「Well, it doesn’t matter. Anyway, you understand right? Intend for all of you to be present in the victory parade. Prepare yourselves accordingly.」

The fact that Rei averted his gaze seemed to show them that they had hit the nail on the head.

Realising that, the guerrilla unit all looked at Rei with gratitude.

「Captain, if you don’t mind, can I come with you when you return to the Mireana Kingdom?」

「Ah, you’re getting ahead of me. Captain Rei, let me come with you as well.」

「You guys……please consider Captain Rei’s position at least. Oh, and I’ll come too, so please take care of me.」

「That’s great……I was thinking of quitting being a soldier and becoming an adventurer instead.」

Rei was stunned by their sudden words.
