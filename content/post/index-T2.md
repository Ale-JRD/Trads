# 805

Shurus’ cavalry and the White Rose Knights.

The two cavalry units were still locked in combat.

The sight of each side searching for an opening in the other looked like a work of art to onlookers.

「Damn it, couldn’t they have just kept watching a little longer?」

「Seriously! Damn it, they’re coming again! Fire, fire!」

The infantry unit that had accompanied the White Rose Knights shouted abuse as they shot their arrows furiously towards the punitive soldiers heading their way.

Until a moment ago, they had been leisurely watching the cavalry battle, but the punitive force suddenly became more active.

They had started to surround the cavalry battle as they attacked the supporting infantry as well.

Of course, this wasn’t to say that Mercurio’s army didn’t know why this had happened.

「There’s no doubt, the Water Dragon is involved!」

As a soldier shouted out, they directed their gaze to where a water dragon had appeared on the battlefield before.

……Yes. Where it had been.

The water dragon that had been there was now no longer visible.

All the soldiers had gasped when they first saw the water dragon and were now relieved that it was gone.

Of course, a water dragon wasn’t something you could find anywhere.

The only person they could think of that could create a water dragon like that was the A rank adventurer Water Dragon Digma, and they had never heard of Digma joining Mercurio’s Army.

In other words, the water dragon had been their enemy.

It wasn’t a strange conclusion to reach.

In addition, Water Dragon Digma was known for using his water dragon in large scale engagements.

The soldiers had been worried about when the water dragon might bare its fangs towards them, but it ultimately seemed to just rampage around where it had first appeared without troubling them.

……That said, where the water dragon did go on a rampage, near where Vihera and Digma had been fighting, both Mercurio’s army and the punitive force had suffered varying levels of casualties.

Fortunately, as the damage was limited to the immediate surroundings, the punitive force soldiers quickly became active.

Water Dragon Digma was nominally one of the punitive force’s most powerful fighters.

But since his water dragon had disappeared without attacking Mercurio’s army, it was only natural for the punitive force to assume that he had been defeated by someone.

In that situation, the punitive force wasn’t left with many options.

Their original plan had been for Digma to play a major role in inflicting casualties on Mercurio’s Army.

This would have reduced the casualties the punitive force would have taken themselves, so it wasn’t a surprising plan.

But since that plan had failed, they decided to take advantage of the fact that they still had four times the number of soldiers in Mercurio’s army.

「Damn it, it looks like we’re being pushed back everywhere! The center front line in particular is being rapidly worn down.」

As the soldier shouted and shot an arrow and turned to look at the center of the battlefield, a calm voice responded.

「The center front line? I believe the soldiers fighting there belong to the traitorous nobles. In that case, there’s no need to worry about them. It’s probably the main camp’s plan to wear them down as well.」

Those words reminded the soldier of past events.

While their own soldiers were being worn down by the punitive force, nearing their limits, he decided that maybe it wasn’t that big of a problem.

To the other soldiers in Mercurio’s army, they deserved it for attempting to betray them.

If they had really been betrayed at a really important point of a battle like this……the losses they would have suffered would likely have determined the outcome of the battle.

「But……regardless, there are just too many of them! There are just too many!」

As the matter of the center front line left the soldier’s mind, he continued to complain about the punitive force soldiers approaching them.

No matter how many arrows they shot, their numbers didn’t seem to drop at all.

Rather, it was their own numbers that were slowly dwindling.

It was in this situation where the difference in numbers became more apparent than ever.

Of course, the White Rose Knights weren’t idle either.

If they lost their supporting infantry, they would be the next to be surrounded.

In addition, to uphold the honour of the White Rose Knights, they couldn’t let the soldiers accompanying them be wiped out either.

However, Shurus’ cavalry weren’t an opponent to be underestimated.

While the White Rose Knights held a very slight advantage in this moment, it was a very precarious balance.

Even the slightest mistake on their part would rapidly shift the advantage to the other side.

(I knew they were strong, but not to this extent. Prince Shurus really does hold great sway over the military.)

Widele thought to herself as she crossed swords with the captain of an opposing cavalry unit.

Her own subordinates also brandished their weapons against the enemy.

Some time had passed since they had first clashed and both sides had suffered injuries. There were even a small number that had been killed in battle.

The main reason why the death toll was so low was primarily due to the fact that both sides had prepared large numbers of potions for the battle.

In terms of amount of potions available to them, the punitive force had more, having support from the Imperial Capital. However, in comparison, the White Rose Knights had a relatively large number of knights in the fifth and sixth units who could use healing magic.

Thanks to that, the White Rose Knights also had minimal casualties.

Of course, even if they were healed with magic, while those with minor injuries could go straight back into battle, this wasn’t possible for serious injuries. Many already had to withdraw from the battlefield because of that.

(If this continues like this, our smaller numbers will be pushed into a corner.)

Widele cut off the tip of a spear that was thrust at her with her magic sword before cutting of right arm of the captain she was fighting, causing them to grit their teeth.

To begin with, Mercurio’s army was close to four times smaller than the punitive force.

It went without saying what the result would be if they both lost numbers at the same rate.

「What wrong? You were the one who challenged me with that level of strength.」

Widele’s fierce shout echoed into the surroundings.

Having just defeated their captain, many of the opposing knights were intimidated by her shout.

The captain was helped to the rear by his subordinates after losing his right arm and collapsing to the ground. But in actual fact, any other captain among the White Rose Knights other than Widele would have had a hard time defeating the captain from Shurus’ cavalry.

Among the captains of the six units in the White Rose Knights, Widele held the greatest individual strength.

While it might have seemed to the others that Widele had easily won, it only served to remind Widele of the strength of their opponents.

(In any case, now is the time to demonstrate our superior strength, even if it is just a pretense. We have to keep it up……)

As Widele contemplated what needed to be done and who to target next, she suddenly noticed a commotion.

However, it wasn’t coming from Mercurio’s army, but the punitive force.

「What’s going on?」

As Widele wondered what was happening, she poured her magic power into her sword, creating lightning as she slashed at the enemy cavalry.

Her lightning infused blade was particularly potent because the enemy was wearing metal armour.

It might have been a different matter if they had been wearing armour made from magic metals, but not even Shurus’ cavalry could afford to equip everyone with such armour because of its rarity.

Widele swung her sword again and again, hoping to hit as many targets as she could.

「GYAAAAAAAAH-!」

「AHHH-!」

「GAGAGAGAGAGAGA-!」

Each time her attacks hit, the targeted knight would scream as their bodies were paralyzed, causing them to become unable to keep up with their horse’s movements and falling off.

This was a tragedy given this was a battlefield between cavalry forces.

Anyone who fell from their horse in such a battle risked being trampled on by friend and foe……in fact, if they weren’t careful, they even risked being trampled by their own horse, having their body and limbs crushed or head smashed in.

「Messenger, messenger!」

As Widele struck down her enemies with her lightning magic sword, a White Rose Knight rapidly rode up to her.

「What’s wrong?! What’s happened?! I saw a commotion coming from the rear of the punitive force. Is it related to that?」

「Yes. It seems that Count Obrisin and Viscount Bugle’s soldiers have launched an attack on the rear of the punitive force’s infantry!」

「……What?」

Widele stopping pouring magic power into her sword as she seemed a little confused.

「Weren’t Vihera-sama’s soldiers meant to be fighting somewhere else……」

「It seems that Vihera-sama is nowhere to be found.」

Struck by a bad premonition, Widele stopped in her tracks.

She thought for a moment that the fact that Vihera was nowhere to be seen meant that she had either been killed or had suffered severe enough injuries to make her unable to continue fighting.

However, she quickly dismissed the idea.

As one of the most skilled fighters in the White Rose Knights, she could sense Vihera’s strength without even fighting. It was clear that someone like Vihera wouldn’t be easily injured.

Unable to think deeply while fighting at the same time, Widele left the front lines to her subordinates as she retreated to the rear.

……Of course, retreating to the rear still meant that she was within the battlefield, just not on the front lines.

「So, am I correct in saying that Gurgast-dono is leading that force?」

「Rather than saying that he’s leading them, it would be more accurate to say that he and his soldiers are just fighting as they please……but yes, you are correct.」

Widele was aware of the unique fighting style of the soldiers Gurgast led.

It was a strange unit that fought as individuals without any thought given towards coordination, yet somehow still achieving results.

Now that they were part of Mercurio’s army, they had additional support from Tilleyle’s archers, but even without support they would have fought the same way.

Naturally, having survived up until this point fighting like that, it was fair to say that every single soldier under Gurgast was an elite.

Still, considering Vihera’s preferred style of combat, Widele couldn’t shake the thought out of her mind.

「Vihera-sama……could she really have been injured and forced to retreat? That can’t be possible.」

「I can’t imagine Vihera-sama retreating due to an injury. In that case, she must have left her unit for some other reason……I mean, Vihera-sama has some bad habits.」

「……That is true.」

Widele nodded after hearing that.

She knew that Vihera loved to fight.

That said, it did seem strange for her to be so honest about her desire to fight the strong in this battle, which would determine the future of Mercurio’s army……

(No, if there was someone that Vihera-sama wanted to fight, perhaps that means that there is someone who is just that strong. If we left someone like that unchecked, the damage we would have suffered would have been considerable. ……In that case……)

As Widele thought of that, the image of the huge water dragon that could be seen across the battlefield flashed through her mind.

She had been surprised when it had first appeared on the battlefield, but it had then barely moved from its position, seemingly fighting someone.

If the water dragon had been able to rampage freely on the battlefield, there was no doubt that Mercurio’s army would have suffered significant casualties.

The original plan had been for Rei to launch a surprise attack at the enemy’s rear, causing a large amount of confusion, but there wasn’t any sign of that happening.

Nothing was guaranteed on the battlefield and many soldiers still believed that they would win with Rei on their side, but it was also true that some people were beginning to lose heart.

Of course, in such a situation, there were also those who wouldn’t let the situation get any worse……

Under those circumstances, it wasn’t hard to guess who had fought the water dragon.

(If that’s the case, it was probably Vihera-sama.)

She wasn’t certain.

But Widele believed it to be the most likely scenario.

「Contact Commander Angela. It’s time to launch an all out attack.」

After asking the messenger to pass on her message, Widele returned to the front lines.

# 806

「UOOOOOOOOOOOH-! Push, push, push! Keep pushing forward with the numbers advantages!」

「Stop them! Defend! Defend! If we allow the enemy to break through the center, the front lines will collapse in an instant!」

「Damn it, what happened to the nobles stationed there!」

「Who cares? They were put on the front lines for betraying us, they might as well be dead……watch out!」

「Uoooooh! I won’t let them! I won’t let them!」

「We will win! We will win! For Prince Mercurio’s sake, we will definitely win!」

「Long live Prince Shurus! Long live Prince Shurus! Long live Prince Shurus!」

「Diediediediediediediediediediediediediedie!」

「Mercurio’s head! Once we take the traitor’s head, this battle will end! Damn it, you’re in the way, get out of the way!」

「Don’t you dare say that! I won’t let you kill the innocent Prince Mercurio! We’ll take the heads of Shrurus and Kabajid, who were the ones to start this war!」

「You dare call Prince Kabajid by his name as a mere foot soldier! You will pay for that disrespect with your lives!」

The center of the front lines was by far the most intense place of fighting between the punitive force and Mercurio’s army.

Until recently, it had been quite back and forth there, but it was no longer the case.

The punitive force had changed their tactics, charging head first through the center, relying on sheer numbers to break through.

Shouts could be heard all across the battlefield, some to raise morale and others to insult the enemy.

But, no matter how hard Mercurio’s army tried to encourage themselves, it couldn’t offset the difference in numbers.

The three traitorous nobles, who had been placed at the center front line, had already been wiped out……in the literal sense. They were all dead along with their soldiers.

They had fought bravely, absolutely not wanting to die, but the difference in numbers had been too great to make up.

Ultimately, one by one, their subordinates fell. And, despite fighting, crying, and wailing, their lives were taken by the soldiers of the punitive force.

Even though they hadn’t wanted to die, the three nobles had still fought bravely, carrying out their duties much better than had been expected.

If they had chosen to serve Mercurio’s honestly, it might have been possible for them to achieve much greater successes.

However, their end was here in the grindstone of the front lines.

As a result, the next force to bear the brunt of the punitive force’s attack was the true Mercurio’s army.

The force that had been sent to supervise the three nobles quickly withdrew as their reinforcements advanced to defend against the punitive force’s attack.

As they defended, the supervisory force, which had fallen back, made their own attack by firing arrows into the enemy’s rear.

Since their role had been to attack the three nobles if they did anything strange, they had no need to change their equipment. They simply retreated behind their reinforcements before regrouping to attack.

Regrouping to attack after falling back might have been difficult for less trained troops, but for the well trained supervisory force, it wasn’t a difficult task.

Rather, the real problem was……

「Hey, is it true that the guerrilla unit has run into problems?」

「Yes, that seems to be the case. That’s why the punitive force has increased the pressure on their side.」

「……But, the guerrilla unit……if we just consider their strength, aren’t they the most elite force we have?」

「I don’t know. But they’re led by Captain Rei, right? I can’t imagine they would do something like betray us.」

「……That’s true.」

The soldiers of the supervisory force exchanged words as they loosed their arrows.

Their thoughts regarding the guerrilla unit weren’t that of betrayal……but doubt.

Normally, hearing that their own soldiers had starting fighting each other would raise suspicion of betrayal.

However, while they were suspicious, it was more doubt.

The main reason was Rei.

Anyone who knew Rei wouldn’t be willing to betray him, no matter the reward offered.

No matter how much money they might be given……even if it was platinum coins, you couldn’t spend it if you weren’t alive.

And for those who knew Rei’s character, or those who didn’t know him well but had a passing understanding of what he had done up until this point, they all knew that betraying Rei was nothing more than suicide.

For that reason, it was hard to believe that even one or two people in the guerrilla unit would betray them, let alone the significant number of them that had started fighting among themselves.

If anything, it was more likely that they had been plotted against by the punitive force successfully……which was correct.

Only those who were wearing the newly made Wyvern leather armour had gone crazy, attacking friend and foe alike.

Of course, everyone knew that the Wyvern leather had come from the Wyverns of the Dragon Knights Rei had defeated.

Even that aside, many soldiers had seen the guerrillas skinning the Wyverns and some had even ate some of the Wyvern meat that had been cooked afterwards.

If they really had been planning something, they wouldn’t have been so obvious about it.

And, above all……

「If Captain Rei really had betrayed us, he wouldn’t have needed to go through all this trouble. He could have just unleashed a fire tornado behind us or charged at us with Overlord’s Armour. That alone would have made us helpless.」

『Indeed.』

The other soldiers in the surroundings voiced their agreement as they continued to shoot arrows at the enemy.

「You guys! Stop talking nonsense and just keep shooting, shooting, and shooting to stop their advance!」

Nodding at their captain’s words, the soldiers stopped talking as they focused on firing arrows.

They could have continued their chatter while shooting arrows, but their fellow soldiers were clashing with the punitive force in a fierce fight.

Considering that, their captain’s words were the right thing to follow.

(That water dragon……it disappeared without causing much damage to us, so someone on our side must have defeated it……there aren’t that many people who could have defeated an A rank adventurer though.)

A soldier from the supervisory force thought to himself as he took a glance at where the huge water dragon had been just earlier.

However, it was no longer there now and his captain would probably scold him if he decided to bring it up, so he just continued to draw his bow.

Back at Mercurio’s main camp. Vihera smiled as she saw Theorem’s shocked look at who she was carrying.

She seemed amused to see the usually calm and collected Theorem look so visibly shocked.

However, anyone who saw who Vihera was carrying would probably say that Theorem’s reaction was natural.

After all, the person Vihera was carrying was Digma, an A rank adventurer.

A rank adventurers were already considered quite inhuman in terms of their abilities.

So it was only natural for everyone to be surprised at the sight of Vihera carrying one in her arms.

Digma was completely unconscious.

This meant that he had been knocked out somehow and it was obvious who had done it.

But, just to be sure……one noble spoke up timidly.

「Vihera-sama, could that person be……」

「Yes. It’s Water Dragon Digma. An A rank adventurer. We met on the battlefield and fought.」

「Y-You fought him……just like that……」

The noble muttered in shock at Vihera’s reply.

The reason was simple. Water Dragon Digma was a renowned adventurer in the Bestir Empire, known for his skill.

His strength was incomparable to that of ordinary adventurers, not the type of opponent you would defeat and then bring back like a souvenir.

「It was a good match up for me, I suppose. I specialise in one on one combat while Digma is better suited to large scale engagements. The fact that I was able to drag him into my specialty was a big deal. ……So, what should we do with him? Can I just leave him here?」

「That would be a problem.」

A noble replied immediately.

The rest silently, but adamantly agreed.

If Vihera were to leave him here, no one present could realistically deal with him.

Theorem was the most skilled person present, but even he didn’t have the confidence to handle an A rank adventurer……especially one with a title like the Water Dragon.

「Even if you say that, we’re being pushed back right now, you know? As long as Rei can’t move, there’s no way I can stay back, right?」

「That’s true, but……」

They were managing right now, but as Vihera said, they were slowly being pushed back.

Ever since the punitive force had started to push through with sheer numbers, Mercurio’s army had been taking increasing casualties.

The only reason they had been able to hold out against an army four times their size was mainly due to their very high morale and the presence of renowned fighters like Rei, Vihera, and the White Rose Knights.

The also had other top tier forces like the Demon Soldiers, the guerrilla unit, and Gurgast.

But, if the situation continued as it was currently going, they would eventually be overwhelmed at one front, and if that happened it could cause the entire army to collapse.

To prevent that from happening, they needed to push the enemy back somehow. Vihera, who was well known for her command skill and personal strength was the perfect person to do that.

It was precisely because she knew this that she announced to all present that she had to return to the battlefield.

But, even knowing that, the people present found it hard to accept.

If Digma were to regain consciousness and go on a rampage, killing Mercurio, they would lose their reason for fighting this war.

That said, if things continued as Vihera said, the was a high chance they would lose regardless.

Since Rei hadn’t achieved the result they had expected, Vihera was the only one they could rely on in this moment.

「……I see. In that case, let’s do this.」

Vihera pondered the noble’s words for a few seconds before laying Digma on the ground and lightly hitting him with a Magic Palm.

「Gah, hah!」

Digma regained consciousness as the magic power raged inside him.

Seeing that, the nobles gasped in shock.

「Vihera-sama, why would you do such a thing!?」

「You went to all the trouble of knocking him out, so don’t try to wake him up now!」

「Call for the soldiers!」

「No need.」

Vihera dismissed one noble’s sudden call for soldiers.

Knowing that the nobles were all still looking at her, Vihera ignored them as she spoke to Digma, who had just opened his eyes.

「So, you’ve regained consciousness. Do you understand your current situation?」

「I……I think I fought you……yes, I lost.」

Digma closed his eyes as he sat back up and recalled what had happened before he lost consciousness. Vihera nodded back.

「That’s right. And then I captured you and took you prisoner.」

Digma nodded back at Vihera’s words.

He remembered losing and he was also now among Vihera’s companions.

As an A rank adventurer, he had also met with Theorem, the man who effectively controlled Mercurio’s army, several times before.

That was why it was impossible for him not to realise that he was now in Mercurio’s camp.

「I will guarantee your life. So, will you behave properly as a prisoner?」

「……What about you, Vihera?」

「I’m one of the main forces in Mercurio’s army. Naturally, I have to return to the battlefield. But, I can’t take you with me. So, as a prisoner, I would like you to behave properly and not do anything strange.」

Vihera’s blunt words made a few of the nobles, who had been listening in, gasp.

Only Mercurio and a few others smiled and laughed.

Theorem had spoken with Digma several times before and was familiar with his personality, so he wasn’t too worried.

Digma thought for a few seconds after hearing Vihera’s words……

「I understand. I lost to you. I could be dead already, but you spared my life. So, it’s only natural that I will obey you for now.」

The eyes of the nobles around them went wide as Digma spoke in a matter of fact tone.

They hadn’t expected Digma to accept it so readily.

(Even if I want to fight Rei with all my might, I can do so after the civil war ends.)

Digma’s original goal for coming to the battlefield was to fight against Rei without any restrictions.

Noiz had taken over that role in this battlefield, but Digma still had hopes he could do so after the civil war.

Of course, Vihera had also saved his life.

「Really? Well then, I believe you, Water Dragon Digma. I’ll leave everything else to you guys.」

Saying that, Vihera returned to the battlefield.

As he watched Vihera’s alluring figure leave, Digma felt something stir within him.

# 807

Rei’s Death Scythe clashed against Noiz’s magic sword.

At the same time, a visible shockwave spread out into the surroundings.

The positions of the two weapons semi-stablised mid clash as both sides tried to overpower the other.

Overlord’s Armour greatly boosted the user’s physical strength.

Rei’s Fire Emperor’s Crimson Armour added the fire attribute to Overlord’s Armour, providing even greater physical enhancement

In terms of effects, Fire Emperor’s Crimson Armour was superior to Overlord’s Armour.

And yet, Rei and Noiz were still fighting on equal terms……because of how well they understood the other’s skills and the extent of which they had mastered their own.

Being the creator of Overlord’s Armour, Noiz had used it for many years.

In contrast, not only had Rei just learned Fire Emperor’s Crimson Armour, even the skill it was derived from, Overlord’s Armour, had only been learned when he had fought against Noiz in the fighting tournament not that long ago.

He hadn’t been taught how to use it either, instead picking it up from watching Noiz use it himself..

As Overlord’s Armour was something Rei had learned on his own, his magic power efficiency when using it was terrible. Even with the vast magic power he had, he couldn’t keep it active for very long.

……All that said, considering he had learned the skill just from watching and being hit by it, it just went to show how good Rei’s combat sense was.

In any case, he had turned Overlord’s Armour into Fire Emperor’s Crimson Armour before he had even mastered Overlord’s Armour.

Using the attribute aligned to his magic power, the skill now used a lot less magic power……but even if he instinctively understood how the skill now functioned, he was still far from mastering it.

That was the main reason he was still unable to defeat Noiz, who was using Overlord’s Armour.

Of course, there were other reasons as well.

「Gururururururu~!」

A cry echoed through the area as the Death Scythe and magic sword clashed against each other, seemingly frozen in time.

At the same time, Noiz quickly pulled his magic sword back before leaping away……and the next moment, several ice arrows pierced the location he had just been standing at.

As he leaped away, Noiz dashed forward again as soon as he landed.

Making a feint against Rei, he suddenly jumped straight up, swinging his sword at Set, who was flapping his wings mid air to hold his position.

Naturally, Set didn’t plan on waiting to be hit and flapped his wings again to dodge.

Normally, Set would have tried to counter with an attack from his own claws, but seeing that Noiz was fighting on equal terms against Rei with regards to physical strength……or even having the slight advantage, Set didn’t dare to be careless, even if he was wearing the Bracelet of Herculean Strength, which boosted his own physical strength.

But, Noiz seemed to know that Set would choose to dodge and swung his magic sword at him, unwilling to let Set fly away……

「Don’t think I’ll let you!」

Rei released a Deep Flame, tracing a path that separated Set from Noiz.

Seeing that, Noiz quickly withdrew his attack.

If he hadn’t given up then, it was clear that the Deep Flame would have hit him.

Fire Emperor’s Crimson Armour was an upgraded version of Overlord’s Armour and Overlord’s Armour was effectively made up of magic power that had been compressed to the point that it was visible.

As for Rei’s magic power……he had an incredibly high aptitude towards the fire attribute, or rather, his only aptitude was towards fire.

As a result, the compressed magic power that surrounded him wasn’t just visible, it would also explode on contact……or at Rei’s will, creating varying explosions of fire in the surrounding area.

As Rei was the only one who could decide how the flames would react on contact, Noiz had no way of knowing what would happen until he got hit.

Using water or ice magic wasn’t really an option for Noiz either as it would be like trying to put out a fire with a cup of water, considering the amount of magic power in Rei’s flames.

That was why Noiz had to avoid the attack.

When Rei was still using the original Overlord’s Armour, it was troublesome to deal with but still possible to handle.

However, Rei’s strength was now at a different level.

As an individual, Noiz still didn’t think it was possible for him to lose, but with Set joining the fight, he couldn’t help but be pushed back.

「Flying Slash!」

As Rei moved towards Noiz, who was falling from the sky, he unleashed a Flying Slash from the Death Scythe.

His Fire Emperor’s Crimson Armour was an incredibly powerful skill, but that same strength was also its weakness.

It could shoot out the compressed magic power that was used to form it, but doing so would also take away some of the magic power around Rei, reducing the direct effect of the skill on him.

In other words, the more he used Deep Flames, the more magic power he would need to compress to make up for the expenditure.

And, each time he had to do that, it meant consuming more magic power.

While it wasn’t as much as when he was using Overlord’s Armour, it was still using a tremendous amount of magic power compared to what an ordinary mage would have, so it couldn’t be considered a skill that could be used for an extended duration.

To compensate, Rei frequently used his other skills like the flying slash he had just sent out.

「Naive! Do you think an attack like that will still work?」

Noiz struck out towards the flying slash heading towards him.

His own attack easily neutralised the flying slash.

However, Rei ignored Noiz’s words as he used Magic Shield to press forward.

「Penetrate, Corrosion, Pain Burst, Power Slash!」

As Rei swung the Death Scythe, he chained several skills in quick succession.

He used Penetrate along side a thrusting attack with the shaft, but Noiz’s magic sword easily deflected it.

Taking advantage of that moment, Rei used Corrosion as the Death Scythe clashed with the magic sword. However, it appeared to have no effect at all, likely due to some passive effect of the magic sword.

The Pain Burst attack was also deflected away by the magic sword as the final Power Slash sent Noiz flying away.

Noiz was blown back around 3m and was about to land safely when……

「Terrain Manipulation!」

Rei thrust the butt of the Death Scythe into the ground as he activated the skill.

The timing was perfect.

It activated just as Noiz’s foot touched the ground as he landed.

Rei’s Terrain Manipulation skill was only at level 1, so he could only raise or lower the ground around him in a 10m radius and only by 10cm up or down.

However, Rei had activated the skill, causing the ground to drop by 10cm just as Noiz felt that he had landed back on the ground, causing him to lose his balance.

Of course, he didn’t spectacularly fall or lose his balance greatly.

However, as this was the first time that Rei had used the skill in this fight, he didn’t even realise what had happened for a moment.
