## 596 - Velmore Forest Ruins (4)

We were fascinated by the scenery around us for a while, and after that, the Sheep Horn Clan members looked very curious about what was to come, but since quite a bit of time had passed, we decided to call it a day for today’s investigation.

Sheep Horn Clan members kept asking with smiles, “How about we start investigating early tomorrow?”

I replied with a wry smile, “I’ll join as early as I can. However, it depends on Sakuya’s mood, so please bear with me on that point,” and we returned to our tent.

Layla was also incredibly excited, and overwhelmed with excitement, she was now clutching my arm tightly and looking a bit fidgety, which made me feel nice.

However, even in that state, her ability to take care of Sakuya perfectly was what made her Layla.

“Yuki-san, those ruins are an enormous discovery! Just the fact that Sakuya helped discover those ruins will be enough to make his name go down in history!” (Layla)

Layla pressed her voluptuous breasts against my arm and hugged me.

It felt great.

“Haha, I see. Good for you, Sakuya. Your name will now be engraved in this world’s history, won’t it?” (Yuki)

“…Well, as expected of Sakuya. Your radar was blazing.” (Enne)

Enne said, gently patting Sakuya, who was already asleep.

“No, seriously. I really didn’t expect such a strong reaction from him.” (Yuki)

I had brought Sakuya all the way here because I thought there would be some kind of reaction, but I honestly didn’t expect it to be this bad.

Shii must have been very surprised when she first encountered such a situation.

“Sakuya, your mother is pleased. Thanks to you, all of our family members are at the peak of happiness.” (Layla)

Layla, who had been clinging to me, then knelt in front of Sakuya’s bed and lightly kissed her son’s forehead.

Yes, it seemed like she was still a little excited. It was really rare for Layla to do something like this.

That was just how Layla was, I was sure that other Sheep Horn Clan members were also excitedly compiling records in their tents.

It was not like they were excited and having a toast because of the discovery, but they were instead excited because they wanted to be the first to make a recording of this discovery! That’s the Sheep Horn Clan for you.

Well, if I was able to help them, I was happy as well.

It was my wife’s family home, so I wanted to get along with them if possible.

◇◇◇

The next day.

I woke up early in the morning, and when Sakuya woke up and was full of energy, we went into the ruins again.

Following the same path as yesterday, we arrived at the underworld, and started walking towards the castle we had seen.

However, my pace was much slower than yesterday.

Since these ruins seemed to be still operating, then it was possible that some sort of defense system was also in operation. Sheep Horn Clan members also shared my opinion.

Also, the words “defense system,” made me remember something else.

The enemy with many arms that shot beams, like the ones guarding the Ancient Ruins in the Demon Forest – the [Ancient Golem].

Or rather, the Asura Golem. A golem that would definitely kill any intruder.

It was not far-fetched to say that there would be something similar here.

That thing was dangerous, if even one of them appeared here, there would be a very high chance that this research team would be annihilated.

When I faced that Asura Golem last time with my team, we gave up on defeating it head on and came up with a plan to somehow avoid it.

…At this point, I was a little scared of not having Rir around.

If he were here, his wild instincts would have helped us sense danger better than I could, which would have made us a little safer.

More than anything, it definitely would make me feel a lot safer.

If Enne was my weapon, Rir was my buddy. My true fighting skills were at their best when these two were together.

…I could not really hold back, right?

“…Sorry, Enne. I can’t let my guard down here.” (Yuki)

\[…Hmm. It can’t be helped. Life is more important.\] (Enne)

After getting her permission, I took out the Divine Spear from my item box.

It was my trump card, but I rarely used it.

Part of it was because I had Enne with me, but I never used the God series weapons as they were intended to since the last war. I think it was better to keep them in storage if possible… In this place, it was not a situation for me to be saying such things.

If we were to fight against something from the Age of Gods, we needed weapons made from the Age of the Gods. It was only that weapon that I deemed suitable for use at the moment.

So I chanted.

“\[Sing of Life, my spear\]” (Yuki)

At that moment, the bone-like spearhead changed all at once, and it even affected my body.

My arm was covered in magic power and patterns, like armor.

However, Enne was still out.

I held Enne in my right hand and \[Ruin\] in my left.

I did not have the strength to control both weapons at the same time. Skills… well, never mind that, just being able to use the two weapons together was more than enough for me right now.

“Well, Demon Lord, that’s…” (Eldogalia)

Seeing my state, Eldogalia-sensei let out a voice of surprise.

Around me, the members of the Sheep Horn Clan were also looking at me, speechless.

“Hmm, yeah, this is my trump card. Sorry, but no questions about this. Oh, you can ask Layla. She’s already asked me everything she could.”

I did answer most of what I could.

Well, the switch between the normal Layla and her other side that came out from time to time made her really cute. She was usually calm and collected, but she got all excited when it came to things she liked.

I got ready, so I asked everyone to wait for a moment and went ahead.

I made wings appear on my back and flew up to check what was ahead from the sky.

If it was just to protect myself, there were many ways to do it, so I should still be safe.

–It seemed that this place wasn’t destroyed in the war.

Judging from the state of the castle town, that was the impression I got.

There was no sign of any conflict. The houses were clean, and there were no collapsed buildings.

No, there were a number of places that seemed to have collapsed due to aging, but that was it.

This place… had probably been deteriorating slowly.

I didn’t think it was destroyed by any dramatic events.

“Enne, what do you think happened here?” (Yuki)

\[…It feels like this place has completed its role.\] (Enne)

“Role… I see. It does feel that way.” (Yuki)

I remembered a conversation with Ruin.

\-The primordial god Dominus sought \[Chaos\].

Chaos was, in short, change and prosperity.

If this place was the \[Beginning\]…it would not be a place that Dominus wanted, would not wish for humans to stay here alone.

So this place was no longer in use…?

As I was flying while thinking about this, I eventually arrived near the castle.

It really was a castle of incredible scale. It was definitely a size bigger than my own demon castle.

If I were to focus on exploring this place completely, it would probably take several months.

For now, there didn’t seem to be any problems, but I didn’t go to call everyone.

My instincts had saved me so many times.

They were telling me.

–Dodge now.

Following my instincts, I immediately stopped hovering and began free-falling.

At that moment, a beam grazed right above me, accompanied by a whoosh that sounded like the air was burning.

“Danger!?”

Several more beams came flying after that, but it wasn’t that difficult to avoid them unless I was caught off guard.

As I drew an evasive trajectory, I turned my gaze towards the source of the beam.

Is that a drone!?

“Is this world actually a sci-fi world!?”

Flying towards me from the direction of the castle were three drones that were dull metallic, but had partially biological movements and appearances, especially a shape reminiscent of a pteranodon.

No, rather than calling them drones, they were probably a type of golem.

As I was thinking about that, I looked at it with my analysis skill, it seemed to be the same [Ancient Golem] type as the Asura Golem from a while ago.

But at this point, this did not resemble fantasy but science fiction. It even shot beams from its mouth.

This was not good.

If I were alone, I would have chosen to retreat without hesitation, but now everyone was far behind.

Now that I’d become a target, retreating like this would make others targets as well.

“I have no choice…!! Enne, I’m going to charge!!” (Yuki)

[…Nnh!] (Enne)

At that moment, I stopped my evasive trajectory and charged in at the shortest distance possible.

I continued to dodge by turning my body in the air, and when I got close to one of the SF Ptera, I first swung my Divine Spear over it.

SF Ptera seemed to have sensed the power of the third form of the Divine Spear at a glance, and immediately moved to dodge it – and then, guiding its movement, I struck it with Enne.

It felt like hitting steel, but Enne’s blade firmly cut SF Ptera’s wing in half.

SF Ptera lost control and fell with a whoosh, crashing into a house in the castle town. There was no reaction.

…It appeared that I destroyed a valuable research resource, but I hoped that others would forgive me.

–Okay, even Enne could cut those things.

“Ha, that’s right, my child is the strongest!!” (Yuki)

Ruin mentioned that Enne had the potential to become a God series weapon someday.

She would not be blocked easily by a mass-produced golem like you!!

Oh, but please no Asura Golems. Those could really kill us.

–From there, a dogfight with the remaining two began.

The SF Ptera moved around in trajectories that would certainly be impossible for living creatures, making sharp turns, sudden stops, and hovering, but it seemed that my flying performance was still superior to theirs.

As we fought, I managed to get behind one of them.

SF Ptera made a sudden stop to catch me off guard, but I saw through its plan, so as I passed it, I pierced it with my Divine Spear, cutting it in half.

Meanwhile, the remaining SF Ptera got behind me and fired a beam, but these golems weren’t the only ones that could make sharp turns.

I spun around and changed the direction of my body by moving my three pairs of wings.

Using the sky as a foothold, I kicked the air to close the distance to the SF Ptera approaching from behind, and while avoiding the beam of the approaching SF Ptera, I cut it in half with a single slash as we passed each other.

A few moments later, I heard a thud from below, the sound of something falling.

There were no additional SF Ptera.

I was the only one left flying in the sky.

In this place particularly… the air was filled with a high concentration of magic. Because of this, it was even harder to tell the difference between golems and the others.

Anyway, I decided to go back for now, and put one of the SF Ptera that I had shot down relatively cleanly as a sample into my item box.

Well, even if they were small, they were still around the size of a person, but no matter how one looked at it, they were designed to be used in numbers.

If even tens of them had appeared, that alone could have been dangerous.

At the very least, I wouldn’t have been able to defeat them so easily.

“I see. As I expected, these are incredibly troublesome. It was hard enough to find the entrance, but it looks like it’s going to be even harder to proceed further.” (Eldogalia)

“Yeah. But honestly, going through here and heading further into the ruins in a conventional way… won’t be impossible, but it will surely be extremely difficult. If the security system activates for some reason, it’ll be a disaster–” (Yuki)

Then, a thought struck me.

… I guess a conventional approach wouldn’t do.

At that moment, I looked at Sakuya.

My son in Layla’s arms seemed to be in a bad mood.

“Layla, how is Sakuya?” (Yuki)

“Yes, he’s still interested in the castle. He wants to go, but he’s sulking and crying because we won’t move.” (Layla)

Ah, I see… so that was why he was in a bad mood.

“–Sakuya, what do you think? Is there somewhere we should go?” (Yuki)

“Ahh! Uh!” (Sakuya)

But Sakuya pointed at the castle, just like Layla said–no, wait.

The first thing we saw in the direction Sakuya pointed was the castle wall. Not the castle itself.

Sakuya’s radar was a mysterious ability that we didn’t understand.

But it certainly was useful.

From what we’d seen so far, it seemed like Sakuya was always pointing at the best path. We were currently finding it hard to deal with the ruins’ security.

It would not be a wise choice to break through straight ahead, unless that was the only option… but with ruins of this size…

Surely, there would be some kind of hidden path, right?

“…Is it worth looking for? Master, should we all try to find something like a hidden path in this direction?” (Emyuu)

“…Well, it’s worth a shot. –Okay, you guys, let’s analyze that toy later! Traces of magic, hidden paths, anything goes, let’s look for it now!” (Eldogalia)

We all went out to look for something in the direction Sakuya was pointing.

And it appeared that my guess was right.

What Sakuya was pointing at wasn’t the castle.

When I picked up my son and flew to the side, the direction of his interest changed.

Checking from three different points, the exact location Sakuya had indicated was not the castle, but a castle wall.

At first glance, it was just a wall, but once Sheep Horn Clan members analyzed it, it was obvious.

“…! This is a secret passage!”

Emyu seemed excited.

As they investigated, the wall disappeared, and a path to the depths suddenly appeared.

The wall was probably cast with some kind of illusion magic.

“…Your son is really outstanding. I didn’t think he’d be this good. Can I study him a bit?” (Eldogalia)

“When Sakuya grows up, please negotiate with him. I won’t be against it.” (Yuki)

“Okay, I got your word. I’m looking forward to his future.” (Eldogalia)

“No, Master. When Sakuya grows up, I’ll study him.” (Emyuu)

“Honestly, I want to study Sakuya too!” (Layla)

“…Sakuya, you’re so popular.” (Enne)

“Haha, that’s true.” (Yuki)

Sakuya, if you went to the Sheep Horn Clan village, you would be really popular. You also looked a lot like Lefi, not me.

But I had to tell you one thing, my son.

It would be difficult to deal with women. Be careful.

◇◇◇

After that, we proceeded carefully through the hidden passage.

It seemed that this place was like a sewer.

That said, since there were no people here, the water was clean and so clear that you could see the bottom. You could probably drink it.

It was dark, but there was enough light to see your feet, provided by mysterious luminous plants, and it was a place that could be called a dungeon.

…But to begin with, do we really need a sewer system here?

It’s the same in my house, but all the wastewater from daily life was absorbed and disappeared into the dungeon. It was probably converted into DP or magic.

So, there was no water or sewer system in my house, and if this place was similar to a dungeon, there shouldn’t be a need for a sewer system.

In that case, I wondered if this sewer system existed to create an atmosphere.

“……” (Yuki)

“? Yuki-san, is there something wrong?” (Layla)

“No… it’s just a little…” (Yuki)

Layla looked at me quizzically as I thought about something.

What I felt at that time was a sense of playfulness.

It gave off the feeling of the creator thinking that maybe it would be fun if something like this existed here.

Just like how I built all kinds of things in the grassland area, thinking that Illuna and the others would enjoy it… it felt like this place was overflowing with such things.

The same went for this secret passage, and the walls that surrounded the castle.

As for whether they were necessary or not… I didn’t think they were necessary at all.

Walls were built in order to protect oneself from enemies. But did the gods have other enemies?

No, I know that the gods have fought each other in the past, but that must have been a later story in the Age of the Gods.

If this place was the \[Origin\], then this town should have existed before that conflict arose, and the walls here were built along with the town as a whole structure.

Basically, what I was trying to say was that this really was the same as our dungeon.

It was not a matter of whether it was necessary or not, it was just that it was built that way because it was more interesting.

Suddenly, I felt like I understood why the people of the Age of the Gods – no, the gods themselves – abandoned this place.

This place was too convenient.

Surely, everything should be here.

But because everything was here, there was no development.

Because everything was here, culture could not develop. If there were no inconveniences, there was no thought to eliminate them.

Just like how the culture of the dragon race, who had long, long lives, had currently stagnated.

Our home was still fine. We enjoy all of this convenience, but our home was, so to speak, just a big house. There was a connection to the outside world, and \[differences\] exist.

But if this became a country… \[chaos\] would not be born here.

Since it was likely the \[origin\], there could not have been any other civilizations before this one.

“So this place really was abandoned.” (Yuki)

This was the beginning, not the end.

And so, with its purpose ending midway… this place was abandoned.
